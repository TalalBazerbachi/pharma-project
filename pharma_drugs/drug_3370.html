Cream, used to aid to the local treatment of muscular and tendino-ligamentous pains.


This medicine MUST NOT BE USED in the following cases: 

  • Allergy to salicylates, to a related medicine (NSAI, Aspirin) or any of the other ingrediants. 
  • Do not apply to mucous membranes, to the eyes, to open,weeping or infected wounds or under an occlusive dressing. 

This medicine MUST NOT BE USED in the following cases, because of the presence of therpenic derivatives in the formula: 

  • infants under the age of 30 months.
  • children with an history of convulsions due to fever or not. 

In case of doubt, it is essential to ask your doctor or your pharmacist for advise. 

Special warning: 

This product contains terpenic derivatives which, at excessive doses, can inculde neurological accidents such as convulsions in infants and children, Use strictly as directed:

  • Never apply more than the prescribed dose ( see "Dosage" )
  • Do not apply to large area of the body. 
  • Comply with the recommended frequency and duration of the treatment. 

Precautions of use: 

This medicine is reserved to adults and children above 7 years old.

After use, It is necessary to wash your hands to pervent accididental contact between the cream and the eyes or mucous membranes. 

Ask your physician for advice in the case of past ot recent epilepsy.

In case of doubt, do not hesitate to ask your doctor or your pharmacist for advice,

Durg interactions and other types if interactions:

In order to avoid any interaction between several medicines, you must systematically infrom your doctor or pharmacist if you are talking any other treatment. 

Pregnancy - Breast- Feeding: 

As a general rule, Always ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking any medicine if you are pregnant or breastfeeding. It is preferable not to use this drug while breastfeeding and it should never be applied to the breasts. 

Excipients which must be specified for the risk -free use of this durg in certain patients: 

Terpenic derivatives ( See "Undesirable and Unpleasant effects" ) 


Dosage: RESERVED TO ADULTS AND CHILDERN ABOVE 7 YEARS OLD. Apply topically by massaging into the painful area. 2 to 3 times per day, until the cream has completely penetrated the skin. Wash hands after use. 

Method and route of administration: Cutaneouse route

Treatment duration: Do not use for prolonged periods without medical advice. 


As with any active substance, this medicine may in some people cause more or less unpleasant effects: Possible local allergic reactions requiring treatment discontinuation. Because of the presence of terpenic derivatives as excipients and when the recommended doses are not respected: 

  • Risk of convulsions in infants and children
  • Possibility of agitation and confusion in elderly subjects 

Do not hesitate to ask your doctor or your pharmacist for advice and report any undesirable or unpleasant effect not mentioned in this leaflet. 


Do not exceed the expiry date indicated on the external packagin. 

To be stored at a temperature below or equal to 25C 

Keep out of the reach and sight of children

Algesal Baume Cream

Each 100 g of cream contains Salicylic acid……………. 6.54 g Diethylamine……………... 3.46 g

Homogenous ivory white cream with characteristic odor of lavender.

Aid to the local treatment of muscular and tendino-ligamentous pains


Reserved to adults and children above 7 years old. Apply topically by massaging into the painful area, 2 to3 times per day, until the cream has completely penetrated the skin. Wash hands after use


Hypersensitivity to the active substances or to any of the excipients. Algesal should not be used if the surface of the skin is broken. Algesal contains terpene derivatives (ie camphor) as excipients, which can lower the epileptogenic threshold and, at excessive doses, lead to neurological accidents such as convulsions in infants and children. Therefore, Algesal should not be used by children who have a history of convulsions. Hypersensitivity to aspirin or other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (including when taken by mouth) especially where associated with a history of asthma.

Consult your doctor before use if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, asthmatic or on any other medicines. 
 
For external use only. Not to be used on broken skin. Avoid contact with eyes and sensitive areas of the skin. Always try on a small area first. Always use sparingly. 
 
Some people may experience discomfort, particularly those with sensitive skin or if used in hot weather/after a hot bath. Temporary skin redness/burning sensation may occur. Discontinue use if excessive irritation or unwanted effects occur. Not to be used on children under 6 years of age. If symptoms persist consult your doctor. Keep all medicines out of the sight and reach of children. 
 
Instruct patients not to smoke or go near naked flames – risk of severe burns. Fabric (clothing, bedding, dressings etc) that has been in contact with this product burns 

more easily and is a serious fire hazard. Washing clothing and bedding may reduce product build-up but not totally remove it


There have been reports that topical salicylates may potentiate the anticoagulant effects of warfarin. It is therefore advisable that caution should be exercised with patients who are on coumarin anticoagulants. Salicylates (as NSAIDs) may increase blood levels and therefore toxicity of methotrexate by delaying its excretion.


Pregnancy: There is inadequate evidence of safety in human pregnancy, Algesal should not be used in pregnancy unless there is no safer alternative. Lactation: Not recommended. 


Not applicable.


Temporary skin reactions (redness, burning sensation and rashes) may occur. 


Overdose is unlikely when used as recommended. If applied to a large area of skin, or in the unlikely event of oral ingestion, the product may cause systemic adverse effects depending on the amount absorbed. Salicylate poisoning is usually associated with plasma concentrations >350 mg/L (2.5 mmol/L). Most adult deaths occur in patients whose concentrations exceed 700 mg/L (5.1 mmol/L). Single doses less than 100 mg/kg are unlikely to cause serious poisoning

 

 


Pharmaco-therapeutic class: Topical analgesic (M: locomotor system) with antiinflammatory properties of salicylates. 


After topical application small amounts of salicylic acid are detectable in the plasma. Elimination in the urine occurs over a period of 48 hours
