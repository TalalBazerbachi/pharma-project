  SFDA PIL (Patient Information Leaflet (PIL) are under review by Saudi Food and Drug Authority)

Zantac belongs to a group of medicines called H2-receptor blockers. It works by reducing the amount of acid in your stomach.

Adults (including the elderly) and children 16 years and older

Zantac is used to:

  • relieve heartburn, acid indigestion (dyspepsia) and for prevention of symptoms associated with consuming food and drink.

Don’t take Zantac

  

  • if you are allergic (hypersensitive) to ranitidine or any other ingredients of Zantac (listed in Section 6).
  • if you are under 16 years of age

è  If you think any of these apply to you, don’t take Zantac until you have checked with your doctor.

 

Take special care with Zantac

 

Talk to your doctor before taking Zantac:

 

  • if you have stomach cancer
  • if you suffer from a rare condition called porphyria
  • if you have kidney disease or liver disease
  • if you are middle aged or older and have dyspeptic (acid indigestion) symptoms for the first time or symptoms that have recently changed
  • if you have lung disease
  • if you are diabetic
  • if you have any problems with your immune system
  • if you have unintended weight loss associated with dyspepsia (acid indigestion)
  • if you are taking pain-killers of the class known as non-steroidal anti-inflammatory (NSAIDS such as aspirin or ibuprofen)

 

è Check with your doctor if you think any of these may apply to you.

 

Other medicines and Zantac

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you're taking any other medicines, if you’ve taken any recently, or if you start taking new ones. This includes medicines bought without a prescription.

 

Some medicines can affect how Zantac works, or make it more likely that you’ll have side effects. Zantac can also affect how some other medicines work. These include:

 

  • warfarin (used to thin the blood)
  • triazolam (used to treat insomnia)
  • glipizide (used to lower blood glucose)
  • midazolam (a sedative that may be given just before an operation)
  • ketoconazole (used to treat fungal infections)
  • atazanavir or delaviridine (used to treat HIV)
  • gefitnib (used to treat lung cancer)
  • Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory (NSAID) medicines (used to treat pain and inflammation).

 

è Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking any of these.

 

Pregnancy and breast-feeding

 

If you are pregnant, or think you could be, or if you are planning to become pregnant, don’t take Zantac without checking with your doctor.  Your doctor will consider the risks and benefits to you and your baby of taking Zantac.

 

The ingredients in Zantac can pass into breast milk.  If you are breast-feeding, you must check with your doctor before you take Zantac.


Always take Zantac exactly as your doctor has told you to. Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you're not sure.

 

How much to take

 

Adults (including the elderly) and children 16 years and older

 

  • One tablet should be taken when symptoms appear, day or night.
  • Most patients find one or two tablets per day sufficient, however, up to 4 tablets may be taken in a 24 hour period.
  • For prevention of symptoms associated with consuming food and drink, swallow one tablet with water, half to one hour beforehand.
  • Do not exceed the recommended dose.
  • Consult a doctor or pharmacist if symptoms persist, get worse or continue for 14 days.

 

How to take

Swallow each tablet whole with some water.

If you forget to take Zantac  

 

If you forget a dose, take it as soon as you remember it, unless it is nearly time for your next dose.  Don't take a double dose.

 

If you take too much Zantac

 

If you take too much Zantac, contact your doctor or pharmacist for advice. If possible, show them the Zantac pack.

Don’t stop Zantac without advice

 

It is important that you take the full course of Zantac. Don’t stop too early, even if you begin to feel better. If you don’t complete the full course, the treatment might not be effective and your symptoms may come back.


Like all medicines, Zantac can cause side effects, but not everybody gets them.

Conditions you need to look out for

 

Severe allergic reaction: These are rare in people taking Zantac. Signs include:

·         raised and itchy rash (hives)

·         swelling, sometimes of the face or mouth (angioedema)

·         chest pain, shortness of breath, unexplained fever, wheezing or difficulty in breathing

·         feeling faint, especially when standing up

·        collapse.

è     Contact a doctor immediately if you get any of these symptoms. Stop taking Zantac.

 

Serious skin reactions: These are very rare in people taking Zantac.

Signs include:

·                     Skin rash, which may blister, and look like small targets (central dark spots surrounded by a paler area, with a dark ring around the edge).

è     Contact a doctor immediately if you get any of these symptoms. Stop taking Zantac.

Rare side effects

These may affect up to 1 in 1,000 people:

·                     allergic reactions

·                     skin rash.

 

Rare side effects that may show up in your blood tests:

·                     changes to liver function.

Very rare side effects

 

These may affect up to 1 in 10,000 people:

·                     inflammation of blood vessels (vasculitis)

·                     inflammation of the pancreas (pancreatitis)

·                     inflammation of the liver (hepatitis), sometimes with yellowing of the whites of the eyes or skin (jaundice)

·                     inflammation in the kidney (interstitial nephritis)

·                     slow or irregular heartbeat

·                     diarrhoea

·                     feeling confused, depressed, or seeing or hearing things that are not really there (hallucinations)

·                     joint or muscle pain, or uncontrolled movement

·                     headache, dizziness, blurred vision

·                     unusual hair loss or thinning (alopecia)

·                     unable to get or maintain an erection (impotence)

·                     unusual secretion of breast milk or breast enlargement in men.

Very rare side effects that may show up in your blood tests:

·                     low levels of white blood cells

·                     decrease in number of blood platelets (cells that help blood to clot)

·                     decrease in number of all types of blood cells.

 

If you get side effects

è Tell your doctor or pharmacist if any of the side effects listed becomes severe or troublesome, or if you notice any side effects not listed in this leaflet.


Keep out of the reach and sight of children.

 

Do not use Zantac after the expiry date shown on the pack.

 

Store below 30°C

Store in the original package to protect from moisture

If you have any unwanted Zantac, don’t dispose of it in your waste water or household rubbish.  Ask your pharmacist how to dispose of medicines no longer required.  This will help to protect the environment.


The active ingredient is ranitidine (as the hydrochloride).

Each tablet contains 75 mg ranitidine hydrochloride.


The 75 mg tablets are packed either in foil strips or double foil blisters.

البالغون (بما في ذلك كبار السن) والأطفال من سن 16 عامًا فما فوق

يُستخدَم زانتاك فيما يلي:

  • تخفيف الحموضة المعوية وعسر الهضم الحمضي والوقاية من الأعراض المرتبطة بتناول الأطعمة والمشروبات.

تجنّب تناول زانتاك في الحالات التالية:

 

  • في حالة الإصابة بفرط الحساسية تجاه الرانيتيدين أو أيٍّ من مكوّنات زانتاك الأخرى (المذكورة في القسم 6).
  • إذا كان عمرك أقل من 16 عامًا

ç    إذا ظننت أن أيًا من هذه الحالات تنطبق عليك، فتجنّب تناول دواء زانتاك إلى أن تستشير الطبيب المعالج.

 

توخّ الحذر عند استخدام زانتاك

 

ينبغي استشارة طبيبك قبل تناول زانتاك:

 

  • إذا كنت مصابًا بسرطان المعدة
  • إذا كنت تعاني من حالة نادرة تسمّى البورفيريا
  • إذا كنت مصابًا بأمراض الكلى أو الكبد
  • إذا كنت في منتصف العمر أو أكبر وتعاني من أعراض عسر الهضم الحمضي للمرة الأولى أو أعراض
    تغيرت مؤخرًا
  • إذا كنت مصابًا بمرض رئوي
  • إذا كنت تعاني من مرض السكري
  • إذا كانت لديك أي مشكلات في جهازك المناعي
  • إذا كنت تفقد وزنك دون تخطيط لذلك بسبب عسر الهضم (عسر الهضم الحمضي)
  • إذا كنت تتناول مسكنات آلام من فئة أدوية تُعرف باسم مضادات الالتهابات غير الستيرويدية (NSAIDS،
    مثل الأسبرين أو الإيبوبروفين)

 

ç    استشر طبيبك إذا كنت ترى أن أيًا من هذه الحالات قد تنطبق عليك.

 

الأدوية الأخرى وزانتاك

أبلغ الطبيب أو الصيدلي إذا كنت تتناول أي أدوية أخرى في الوقت الحالي أو تناولت أي أدوية مؤخرًا أو عند الشروع في تناول أدوية جديدة. ويشمل ذلك الأدوية التي يتم شراؤها دون وصفة طبية.

 

قد تؤثر بعض الأدوية على كيفية عمل زانتاك أو يمكن أن تزيد من احتمال التعرّض للآثار الجانبية. يمكن أن يؤثر زانتاك على كيفية عمل بعض الأدوية الأخرى. وتشمل تلك الأدوية:

 

  • وارفارين (المُستخدَم لترقيق الدم)
  • تريازولام (المُستخدَم لعلاج الأرق)
  • غليبيزيد (المُستخدَم لخفض نسبة الجلوكوز في الدم)
  • ميدازولام (مهدئ يمكن إعطاؤه قبل إجراء العمليات مباشرةً)
  • كيتوكونازول (المُستخدَم لعلاج العدوى الفطرية)
  • أتازانافير أو ديلافيردين (المُستخدَمان لعلاج فيروس نقص المناعة البشرية)
  • جيفيتنيب (المُستخدَم لعلاج سرطان الرئة)
  • أدوية مضادات الالتهابات غير الستيرويدية (NSAID) (المُستخدَمة لعلاج الألم والاتهاب).

 

ç    ينبغي إخبار الطبيب أو الصيدلي إذا كنت تتناول أيًا من هذه الأدوية.

 

الحمل والرضاعة الطبيعية

 

إذا كنتِ حاملاً، أو تظنين أنكِ ربما تكونين حاملاً، أو تنوين الحمل، فلا ينبغي استخدام زانتاك دون استشارة الطبيب.  سوف يقدّر الطبيب المخاطر والفوائد التي تعود عليكِ وطفلك عند تناول زانتاك.

 

يمكن كذلك انتقال مكوّنات دواء زانتاك للطفل من خلال لبن الأم. وإذا كنتِ ترضعين طفلك طبيعيًا، فعليكِ استشارة الطبيب قبل تناول زانتاك.

الجرعة

 

البالغون (بما في ذلك كبار السن) والأطفال من سن 16 عامًا فما فوق

 

  • ينبغي أخذ قرص عند ظهور الأعراض نهارًا أو مساءً.
  • بالنسبة لمعظم المرضى، يكون أخذ قرص أو قرصين يوميًا كافيًا ولكن يمكن أخذ ما يصل إلى 4 أقراص خلال فترة تبلغ 24 ساعة.
  • للوقاية من الأعراض المرتبطة بتناول الأطعمة والمشروبات، ابتلع قرصًا واحدًا مع الماء قبل التناول بفترة تتراوح بين نصف ساعة وساعة كاملة.
  • لا تتجاوز الجرعة الموصى بها.
  • استشر طبيبًا أو صيدليًا إذا استمرت الأعراض أو تفاقمت أو امتدت 14 يومًا.

 

كيفية تناول الجرعة

ابتلع قرصًا كاملاً مع بعض الماء.

في حالة نسيان تناول جرعة زانتاك 

 

في حالة نسيان تناول جرعة، يجب أخذها فور التذكّر، ما لم يكن وقت جرعتك التالية قد حان تقريبًا. تجنّب تناول
جرعة مضاعفة.

 

في حالة تناول جرعة زائدة من زانتاك

 

إذا تناولت جرعة زائدة من زانتاك، يجب استشارة الطبيب أو الصيدلي. ويُفضّل أن تريهما عبوة زانتاك
إن أمكن.

لا تتوقف عن تناول زانتاك دون استشارة المختص

 

من المهم إتمام المسار العلاجي الكامل بزانتاك. لا تتوقف عن تناول الدواء قبل الوقت المحدد بكثير حتى لو بدأت تشعر بتحسن. إذا لم تكمل المسار العلاجي بالكامل، فقد لا يكون العلاج فعالاً وقد تعود الأعراض.

كما هي الحال في جميع الأدوية، يمكن أن يتسبب زانتاك في حدوث بعض الآثار الجانبية، لكن ليس بالضرورة أن يصاب بها جميع الأشخاص.

حالات ينبغي الانتباه لها

 

تفاعلات الحساسية الحادة: هذه التفاعلات نادرة في حالة الأشخاص الذين يتناولون زانتاك. تشمل العلامات:

·         ‏‫طفح جلدي بارز ومثير للحكة (شرى)

·         تورم يصيب الوجه أو الفم أحيانًا (وذمة وعائية)

·         ألم في الصدر أو ضيق في التنفس أو حمّى مجهولة السبب أو أزيز أو صعوبة أثناء التنفس

·         الشعور بالإغماء، ولا سيما عند الوقوف

·        الإغماء.

ç                    يجب الاتصال بالطبيب على الفور إذا أصابتك أي من هذه الأعراض. توقّف عن تناول زانتاك.

 

التفاعلات الخطيرة للجلد: يندر جدًا إصابة المرضى الذين يستخدمون زانتاك بها.

تشمل العلامات:

·                     الطفح الجلدي الذي قد يتقرّح ويشبه الأجزاء الناتئة الصغيرة (بقع سوداء مركزية مُحاطة بمساحة شاحبة، مع وجود حلقة داكنة حول الأطراف).

ç                    يجب الاتصال بالطبيب على الفور إذا أصابتك أي من هذه الأعراض. توقّف عن تناول زانتاك.

 Read this leaflet carefully before you start using this product as it contains important information for you

ZANTAC Tablet Ranitidine 75 mg

Zantac 75 mg tablets: Each tablet contains 75 mg ranitidine as the hydrochloride.

Tablets

  • Adults (including the elderly) and children 16 years and older

Symptomatic relief of heartburn, dyspepsia (acid indigestion) and hyperacidity, and for prevention of symptoms associated with consuming food and drink.


Adults (including the elderly) and children 16 years and older

One tablet should be taken when symptoms appear, day or night. Most patients find one or two tablets per day sufficient, however, up to 4 tablets may be taken in a 24 hour period.

For prevention of symptoms associated with consuming food and drink, swallow one tablet with water, half to one hour beforehand.

 

Populations

·         Renal Impairment

Adults (including the elderly) and children 16 years and older

Ranitidine is excreted via the kidney and so plasma levels of the drug are increased in patients with renal impairment (creatinine clearance less than 50 ml/min). Ranitidine products are not suitable for these patients without medical supervision.


ZANTAC products are contraindicated in patients known to have hypersensitivity to any component of the preparation.

Ranitidine products are not suitable for patients with renal impairment (creatinine clearance less than 50 ml/min) without medical supervision.

Rare clinical reports suggest that ranitidine may precipitate acute porphyric attacks. ZANTAC should therefore be avoided in patients with a history of acute porphyria.

Although clinical reports of acute intermittent porphyria associated with rantidine administration have been rare and inconclusive, rantidine should be avoided in patients with a history of this condition.

Regular supervision of patients who are taking non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs concomitantly with oral ZANTAC is recommended, especially in the elderly and in those with a history of peptic ulcer.

In patients such as the elderly, persons with chronic lung disease, diabetes or the immunocompromised, there may be an increased risk of developing community acquired pneumonia. A large epidemiological study showed an increased

risk of developing community acquired pneumonia in current users of H2 receptor antagonists versus those who had stopped treatment, with an observed adjusted relative risk increase of 1.63 (95% CI, 1.07 - 2.48).

As ZANTAC effervescent tablets contain aspartame they should be used with caution in patients with phenylketonuria. ZANTAC effervescent tablets contain sodium (see List of Excipients for sodium content). Care should therefore be taken in treating patients in whom sodium restriction is indicated.

Bradycardia in association with rapid administration of ZANTAC injection has been reported rarely, usually in patients with factors predisposing to cardiac rhythm disturbances. Recommended rates of administration should not be exceeded.

The use of higher than recommended doses of i.v. H2- antagonists has been associated with rises in liver enzymes when treatment has been extended beyond five days.

Treatment with a histamine H2-antagonist may mask symptoms associated with carcinoma of the stomach and may therefore delay the diagnosis of the condition. Accordingly, where gastric ulcer has been diagnosed or in patient of middle age and over with new or recently changed dyspeptic symptoms the possibility of malignancy shoulb be excluded before therapy with ZANTAC tablets is instituted.


Ranitidine has the potential to affect the absorption, metabolism or renal excretion of other drugs.  The altered pharmacokinetics may necessitate dosage adjustment of the affected drug or discontinuation of treatment.

 

Interactions occur by several mechanisms including:

 

1) Inhibition of cytochrome P450-linked mixed function oxygenase system:

Ranitidine at usual therapeutic doses does not potentiate the actions of drugs which are inactivated by this enzyme system such as diazepam, lidocaine, phenytoin, propranolol and theophylline. 

There have been reports of altered prothrombin time with coumarin anticoagulants (e.g. warfarin). Due to the narrow therapeutic index, close monitoring of increased or decreased prothrombin time is recommended during concurrent treatment with ranitidine.

 

2) Alteration of gastric pH:

The bioavailability of certain drugs may be affected.  This can result in either an increase in absorption (e.g. triazolam, midazolam, glipizide) or a decrease in absorption (e.g. ketoconazole, atazanavir, delaviridine, gefitnib).

 

There is no evidence of an interaction between oral ranitidine and amoxicillin and metronidazole.

If high doses (2 g) of sucralfate are co-administered with oral ranitidine the absorption of the latter may be reduced. This effect is not seen if sucralfate is taken after an interval of 2 hours.


Fertility

There are no data on the effects of ranitidine on human fertility. There were no effects on male and female fertility in animal studies (see Pre-Clinical Safety Data).

Pregnancy and lactation

Ranitidine crosses the placenta and is excreted in breast milk. Like other drugs ZANTAC should only be used during pregnancy or during breast-feeding if considered essential.

Pharmacodynamic Properties

ATC Code

Pharmacotherapeutic group: H2-receptor antagonists, ATC code: A02BA02.

Mechanism of Action

Ranitidine is a specific, rapidly acting histamine H2-antagonist. It inhibits basal and stimulated secretion of gastric acid, reducing both the volume and the acid and pepsin content of the secretion.

Pharmacodynamic Effects

Ranitidine has a relatively long duration of action and so a single 150 mg dose effectively suppresses gastric acid secretion for 12 hours. Clinical evidence has shown that ranitidine combined with amoxicillin and metronidazole eradicates Helicobacter pylori in approximately 90% of patients. This combination therapy has been shown to significantly reduce duodenal ulcer recurrence.

Helicobacter pylori infects about 95% of patients with duodenal ulcer and 80% of patients with gastric ulcer.


Absorption

Following oral administration of 150 mg ranitidine, maximum plasma concentrations (300 to 550 ng/mL) occurred after 1-3 hours. Two distinct peaks or a plateau in the absorption phase result from reabsorption of drug excreted into the intestine.  The absolute bioavailability of ranitidine is 50-60%, and plasma concentrations increase proportionally with increasing dose up to 300 mg.

Distribution

Ranitidine is not extensively bound to plasma proteins (15%), but exhibits a large volume of distribution ranging from 96 to 142 L.

Metabolism

Ranitidine is not extensively metabolised. The fraction of the dose recovered as metabolites is similar after both oral and i.v. dosing; and includes 6% of the dose in urine as the N-oxide, 2% as the S-oxide, 2% as desmethylranitidine and 1 to 2% as the furoic acid analogue.

Elimination

Plasma concentrations decline bi-exponentially, with a terminal half-life of 2-3 hours.  The major route of elimination is renal.  After i.v. administration of 150 mg 3H-ranitidine, 98% of the dose was recovered, including 5% in faeces and 93% in urine, of which 70% was unchanged parent drug.  After oral administration of 150 mg 3H-ranitidine, 96% of the dose was recovered, 26% in faeces and 70% in urine of which 35% was unchanged parent drug.  Less than 3% of the dose is excreted in bile.  Renal clearance is approximately 500 mL/min, which exceeds glomerular filtration indicating net renal tubular secretion.

Special Patient Populations

  • Children/Infants (1 month to 11 years)

Limited pharmacokinetic data have shown that the half-life (2-3 hours) and plasma clearance (9-13 ml/min/kg) in children 1 month and above are similar to those for healthy adults receiving oral ranitidine.

  • Patients over 50 years of age

In patients over 50 years of age, half-life is prolonged (3-4 h) and clearance is reduced, consistent with the age-related decline of renal function.  However, systemic exposure and accumulation are 50% higher.  This difference exceeds the effect of declining renal function, and indicates increased bioavailability in older patients.
