  SFDA PIL (Patient Information Leaflet (PIL) are under review by Saudi Food and Drug Authority)

This medicinal product is intended for local symptomatic treatment and relief of pain in the following diseases of the middle ear without tympanic perforation:

-       Acute inflammation of the middle ear of viral or bacterial origin, mainly in the initial stage

-       inflammation of the ear drum

-       Barotraumatic otitis .

Phenazone has a pronounced anti-inflammatory effect and relieves ear pain.

Benzocaine enhances the analgesic effect (pain relief) of phenazone and as a local anesthetic.

The product is intended for adults, adolescents and children from 1 month of age.


Do not use Earcalm

-       if you are allergic to the active substances or any of the other ingredients of this medicine (listed in section 6).

-       if you have perforated or burst ear drum (tympanic perforation) (see Section: Warnings and precautions).

If the conditions mentioned in this paragraph occur only during the use of the product, tell your treating physician.

The product is not intended for newborns (up to 1 month of age).

if you have any doubt, seek advice of your doctor.

 

Warnings and precautions

-       If you have perforated ear drum membrane this medicine may cause side effects in the middle ear. Except ear pain, other common symptoms of perforated ear drum are: vertigo (spinning sensation); hearing change or loss, often with ringing, buzzing, or clicking; fluid or blood draining from the ear. In case of any doubts, as a precautionary measure, it is recommended to visit a doctor to check if your tympanic membrane (ear drum) is intact, before using this medicine.

-       If in doubt, do not hesitate to seek the advice of your doctor or your pharmacist.

Other medicines and Earcalm

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are using, have recently used or might use any other medicines.

The effects of Earcalm and the effects of other concomitant medications may interfere with each other influence.

For Earcalm, interactions are not yet known.

Pregnancy, breast-feeding and fertility

If you are pregnant or breast-feeding, you think you may be pregnant or planning to become pregnant, talk to your doctor before taking this medicine.

The doctor will decide whether to use the product in pregnant or breastfeeding women.

Driving and using machines

Earcalm does not affect the ability to drive and use machines.

 


Always use this medicine exactly as described in this leaflet. Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you are not sure.

The usual dose is 4 drops 2 to 3 times a day in the ear canal of the patient.

Before use warm the ear drops to 37 °C (approximately body temperature). This warming can be done in the palms.

The maximum daily dose should not exceed 12 drops / sore ear per day.

The recommended treatment time should not exceed 5 days.

If after the above treatment symptoms does not improve or it gets worse or if you experience side effects, Consult your doctor for further use.

Instructions for use

-       Unscrew the bottle cap.

-       Turn the bottle down and gently squeeze until a drop is formed.

-       Press again until you obtain the required number of drops.

-       Place back the cap after use.

Earcalm ear drops is a medicinal product intended for local use in the ear.

Do not get the ear drops in the nose or eyes!

Do not get the ear drops through the mouth!

If you use more Earcalm than you should

There are no reports of overdose cases, when Earcalm is properly used.

Consult your doctor.

If you forget to use Earcalm

Do not use a double dose to make up for a missed one.

If you have any further questions on the use of this medicine, ask your doctor or pharmacist

 


Like all medicines, EARCALM can cause side effects, although not everybody gets them.

In rare cases, there is a risk of local reactions such as allergy, irritation, or congestion the ear canal.

If you get any side effects, tell your doctor or pharmacist. This includes any possible side effects not listed in this leaflet.


- Keep out of reach and sight of children.

- Store below 30°C.

- Keep in the original pack to protect from light.

- To be used within 28 days  after first opening.

- Do not use Earcalm after the expiry date which is stated on the bottle and on the carton the bottle is packed in.

- Do not throw away any medicines via wastewater or household waste. Ask your pharmacist how to throw away medicines you no longer use. These measures will help protect the environment.

each 1 ml contains Phenazone  (BP) 50mg &Benzocaine (BP)10mg.

Other ingredients : Propylene glycol.

EarCalm Ear Drops .

Phenazone 5% w/v Benzocaine 1% w/v

Ear Drops EARCALM is clear, colourless to slightly yellow solution free from particles.

Local symptomatic treatment of middle ear pain with intact drum:

  • otitis media acute viral or bacterial etiology
  • myringitis
  • Barotraumatic otitis

The product is intended for adults, adolescents and children from 1 month of age.


The preparation is applied to the external auditory canal. Before use, it is advisable to warm the solution to a temperature 37 °C. This warming can be done in the palms.

The recommended dosage is 4 drops into the outer ear canal 2 to 3 times a day.

Recommended time

treatment should not exceed 5 days.

The maximum daily dose should not exceed 12 drops / sore ear per day.

Hepatic or renal impairment

There is no dose limitation for patients with hepatic or renal impairment.

How to use :

-       Unscrew the bottle cap.

-       Turn the bottle down and gently squeeze until a drop is formed.

-       Press again until you obtain the required number of drops.

-       Place back the white protective cap after use.


This medicinal product should not be administered in the following cases: - Hypersensitivity to the active substances or to any of the excipients listed in section 6.1; - Infectious or traumatic perforation of the tympanic membrane (see section 4.4).

Before the beginning of treatment with the medicinal product, it is recommended to check if there is perforation of the tympanic membrane. If the tympanic membrane is perforated, intra-auricular administration may lead to contact of the product with middle ear structures and cause undesirable effects in these tissues.

with subsequent irritation and the risk of dizziness, aseptic or septic labyrinthitis with consequent irreversible deafness


No interaction studies have been performed


Pregnancy:

No well-controlled studies in pregnant women have been performed.

This product should therefore only be used in pregnant women when there is a potential benefit for the mother authorizes the potential risk to the fetus.

Breast feeding:

Use of this product in breast-feeding women requires caution.

 


Earcalm does not affect the ability to drive and use machines
