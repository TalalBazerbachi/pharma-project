Decozal Child Nasal Spray is for application in the nose to give relief from:

• Nasal congestion (blocked nose, including colds)

• Perennial and allergic rhinitis (recurring inflammation of the nasal mucous membranes, including hay fever).

• Sinusitis.

Decozal contains the active ingredient Xylometazoline hydrochloride which helps to open up and clear the nasal passages by reducing the excessive nasal secretions and returning the swollen blood vessels to their normal size.


Do not use Decozal if your child:

• is under 6 years old

• is allergic to Xylometazoline hydrochloride or any of the other ingredients in the spray (see Section 6)

• has an inflamed nasal passage

• is allergic to other decongestants

• has had recent neurosurgery or nasal surgery.

• has heart disease or hypertension

• has diabetes

• has hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid gland)

• has closed angle glaucoma

• has phaeochromocytoma (a tumour of the nerves associated with the hormone glands)

• is taking monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) or has stopped taking them in the last 14 days

• is taking betablockers

Take special care with Decozal 0.05 % Nasal Spray Tell your doctor if your child:

• has heart or circulatory disease

• is taking any other medicine or is under doctor's care

If your child experiences hallucinations, restlessness or sleep disturbances stop using Decozal.

Do not take Decozal 0.05% Nasal Spray for more than 5 days in a row.

Keep away from eyes.

Using other medicines:

Do not use this spray if your child is using other decongestants. Please tell your doctor or pharmacist if your child is taking or has recently taken any medicines (especially antidepressants, moclobemide, medicines for high blood pressure or heart problems, appetite suppressants, oxytocin or ergot alkaloids) including those obtained without a prescription.


Method for using the metered-dose spray:

-Remove protective cap. Before using for the first time, pump several times (fig.1) until an even mist appears.

-Place the nozzle of the device in the nostril and exert pressure firmly, once only, on the

collar of the metered-dose nasal spray (fig.2).

Do not use in children under 6 years of age.

Administration:        

1. Clear and wipe your child's nose

2. Tilt your child's head right back

3. Apply sprays to each nostril as per dosage instructions.

Parents should seek medical attention if the child's condition worsens.

To avoid possible spread of infection, this spray should only be used by one person.

Do not exceed the recommended dosage.

Do not take the medicine with any other cough and cold medicines.

If anyone has too much

If you use more Decozal Child Nasal Spray than you should, consult your doctor or pharmacist.


Like all medicines, Decozal Nasal Spray can cause side effects, although not everybody gets them. Local discomfort may be felt after applying the spray if you have sensitive nasal passages.

If your child experiences hallucinations, restlessness or sleep disturbances stop using Decozal Nasal Spray.

Occasionally the following side effects have been reported:

• A burning sensation in the nose and throat

• Local irritation

• Nausea

• Headache

• Dryness of the nasal membranes.

Palpitations, changes in heart rhythm or increased blood pressure may also occur, especially if the patient have heart problems.

If any of the side effects gets serious, or if you notice any side effects not listed in this leaflet, please tell your doctor or pharmacist.

Reporting of side effects

If you get any side effects, talk to your doctor or pharmacist. This includes any possible side effects not listed in this leaflet. You can refer to the address of the NPC in Saudi Arabia to report any side effects you get.


- Keep out of the reach and sight of children.

- Do not store above 30°C. Discard the bottle four weeks after opening.

- Do not use Decozal after the expiry date which is stated on the box.

- Do not throw away any medicines via wastewater or household waste. Ask your pharmacist how to throw away medicines you no longer use. These measures will help protect the environment.


The active ingredient in Decozal is: Xylometazoline HCL

Other ingredients are: Benzalkonium chloride 0.01% as preservative, Sodium phosphate monobasic, Sodium phosphate dibasic, Hypermelose, Sodium chloride and highly purified water.


Presentation: 10 ml plastic spray bottle.

 Read this leaflet carefully before you start using this product as it contains important information for you

Decozal Nasal Drops & Decozal Metered Dose Nasal Spray.

Decozal 0.05% Nasal drops, nasal solution contains 0.05% of xylometazoline hydrochloride. Decozal 0.1% Nasal drops, nasal solution contains 0.1% of xylometazoline hydrochloride. Decozal 0.05% Metered Dose Nasal Spray, contains 0.05% of xylometazoline hydrochloride. Decozal 0.1% Metered Dose Nasal Spray, contains 0.1% of xylometazoline hydrochloride. Each metered-dose spray delivers 0.14 mg of xylometazoline hydrochloride. For full list of excipients, see section 6.1.

Nasal Drops, Nasal Spray (solution)

They (nasal drops as well as nasal sprays) are used for various types of colds. It is designed to be applied in the nostrils, where it exerts a vasoconstricting effect thereby decongesting the mucosae of the nose and the throat. This effect allows patients with colds to breathe more easily through the nose. The action of these products is felt within a few minutes and lasts for several hours. They contain ingredients which help prevent the nasal mucosa drying out.  


Unless otherwise prescribed by the doctor, you must keep to the following dosage recommendations:

Infants and children under 2 years old, only under medical prescription, and children under 6 years old under adult supervision: 0.05% nasal drops and metered dose spray:

1-2 drops of solution or 1 spray puff with the 0.05% metered dose spray 1-2 times a day in each nostril are usually sufficient. Do not exceed 3 applications per day.

Adults and children over 6 years: 0.1% nasal drops and metered dose spray: 2-3 drops of solution or 1 spray puff from 0.1% metered dose spray in each nostril generally 4 applications per day are sufficient. The last application will usually take place shortly before bedtime.

Method for using the metered dose spray:

Remove protective cap. Before using for the first time, pump several times until an even mist appears. Place the nozzle of the device in the nostril and exert pressure firmly, once only, on the collar of the metered dose nasal spray.

For subsequent applications, the metered-dose spray is instantly ready for use without prior pumping. Replace the protective cap after use. Please keep to the doses which appear in the package leaflet or prescribed by your doctor. If you feel that the medicinal product is not effective enough or on the contrary, it is too effective, please consult your doctor, or pharmacist. 


If you have had surgery involving the head, it is up to your doctor to decide whether you can use Decozal. This limitation also applies to all other vasoconstrictive products used in the nose. Decozal must not be used in the case of dry nasal mucosa (rhinitis sicca) or in the case of acute (closed-angle) glaucoma. Patients who are allergic to any of the ingredients in Decozal must not use this medicinal product.

As with other medicinal products for colds, Decozal can lead to sleep and vision problems or episodes of dizziness which are evidence of oversensitivity to the product. Please consult the doctor if:

- These effects are very disruptive.

- The cold persists or gets worse.

- Other problems appear.

Decozal must be used with care in the case of hypertension, cardio-vascular disease, thyroid hyperactivity, or diabetes (Diabetes mellitus).

Decozal must not be used for more than a week without medical advice because over a longer period of use, rebound congestion of the nasal mucosa (rhinitis medicamentosa) may appear. This would be very similar to the symptoms of a cold. Do not use the 0.1% drops or metered dose spray in children under 6 years old.

Decozal 0.05% nasal drop and metered dose spray must not be used without a medical prescription in children under 2 years old and must be administered under adults supervision in children under 6 years old.

The doctor must be informed if the patient suffer from another medical condition, allergy or if the patient is already taking or using other medicinal products either internally or externally (even as self-medication).


As for all sympathomimetics, a reinforcement of the systemic effects of xylometazoline by concomitant use of monoamine oxidase inhibitors, tricyclic or tetracyclic antidepressants, cannot be excluded, especially in case of overdose.


No fetal toxicity or fertility studies have been carried out in animals. In view of its potential systemic vasoconstrictor effect, it is advisable to take the precaution of not using Decozal during pregnancy.

Label warning: If you are pregnant or taking any other medicines, or are under a doctor's care, consult him before using Decozal.             

No evidence of any adverse effect on the breast-fed infant. However, it is not known if xylometazoline is excreted in breast milk, therefore caution should be exercised and Decozal should be used only on prescription whilst breastfeeding.


None.


Using Decozal can cause the following side-effects: occasionally, a burning sensation in the nose and throat, local irritation, nausea, headaches and dryness of the nasal mucosa. In very rare cases insomnia or allergic reactions with respiratory problems may also appear.


In rare instances of accidental poisoning in children, the clinical picture has been marked chiefly by signs such as acceleration and irregularity of the pulse, elevated blood pressure and sometimes clouding of consciousness.

There is no specific treatment. Appropriate supportive measures should be initiated.


Decozal contains Xylometazoline which is a sympathomimetic agent with marked alpha-adrenergic activity, and is intended for use in the nose. It constricts the nasal blood vessels, thereby decongesting the mucosa of the nose and neighboring regions of the pharynx. This enables patients suffering from colds to breathe more easily through the nose. The effect of Decozal begins within a few minutes and lasts for up to 10 hours. Decozal is generally well tolerated and does not impair the function of ciliated epithelium.

In a double-blind, saline solution controlled study in patients with common cold, the decongestant effect of Decozal was significantly superior (p<0.0001) to saline solution based on rhinomanometry measurement at 1 hour after administration of the study drugs.


Systemic absorption may occur following nasal application of Xylometazoline hydrochloride solutions. It is not used systemically.
