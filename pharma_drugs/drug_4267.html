Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo is used on the scalp to treat dandruff-mild seborrhoeic dermatitis. It relieves itching scalp,
reduces inflammation and the scaling and flaking of dandruff.

It is effective yet gentle enough for regular use and will not damage permed, bleached and tinted hair.


Do not use Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo if:
I. You have ever had an allergic reaction to miconazole, salicylic acid or phenethyl alcohol;
2. The packaging is torn or the tamper evident seal is broken.
3. The expiry date printed on the pack has passed.

Do not use Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo on children unless your doctor or pharmacist tells you.

Pregnancy and breastfeeding
The safety of miconazole is well reported and, when used in topical medication, is a category A drug as defined in 'Prescribing Medicines in Pregnancy-4th edition' approved by the Australian Drug Evaluation Committee.
Category A drugs are those which have been taken by a large number of pregnant women and women of child-bearing age without any
proven increase in the frequency of malformations or other direct or indirect harmful effects on the foetus having been observed.


Always use Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo exactly as your doctor or pharmacist has told you. You should check with your doctor or pharmacist if you are not sure.
How to use it:
Wet hair and scalp thoroughly. Apply a small amount onto the scalp and massage well. Rinse and repeat using a larger amount. Work into a rich lather. Leave on for 3 -5 minutes. Rinse thoroughly.

Avoid eyes. Do not swallow. If later enters eyes, rinse with clean water.

If irritation occurs, discontinue use.
If you use it less often than you should, it may not work as well and
your scalp problem may not improve. Using it more often than you should may not improve your scalp problem any faster and may cause or increase side effects.

How long to use it:
Use 2-3 times weekly for the first 4 weeks, then once a week to maintain control or to help prevent recurrence.

If you have any further questions on the use of this product ask your doctor or pharmacist



Like all medicines, Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo can cause
side effects, although not everybody gets them.
Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo helps most people with scalp
problems but it may have unwanted side effects in a few people.
Side effects that have been reported by some people include local
irritations such as burning or irritation.
If any of the side effects gets serious, or if you notice any side effects
not listed in this leaflet, please tell your doctor or pharmacist.


Keep Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo out of reach and sight of children.
Keep Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo in a cool dry place where the temperature stays below 30° C.
Do not leave Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo in the car or on window sills. Heat can destroy some medicines
Do not use Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo after the expiry date.


What Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo contains:
Active ingredient: Miconazole 2% w/w
Other ingredients: Citric Acid, Disodium Cocoamphodiacetate, Disodium Laureth Sulfosuccinate, Fragrance, Lauryl Betaine, Phenethyl Alcohol, Salicylic Acid, Sodium Laureth Sulfate, Water- Purified


Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo is a clear very slightly viscous sudsing liquid solution in a 125ml bottle, packed in a carton.

Pharmacotherapeutic group: Imidazole and triazole derivative, ATC code: D01AC02.
Hairscience For Dandruff Shampoo contains miconazole, a synthetic imidazole derivative which acts on the causative agents of topical fungal infections. Miconazole is an antifungal agent and, like other imidazole derivatives, acts by altering the permeability of the cell membrane in sensitive fungi. Minimum inhibitory concentrations for most fungi typically vary between 0.1 and 10 μg/ml. The absorption of miconazole is not significant when topically applied.

Miconazole’s topical applications have been subject to a considerable number of clinical trials. Miconazole is an effective anti-fungal agent, active against a wide range of organisms responsible for diseases at various dermatological sites. A vast amount of work has been done since 1975 and this confirms and extends the initial findings. Miconazole is still highly regarded in medicine as an effective anti-fungal treatment.
Miconazole products in various dose forms, have been used effectively with high degree of safety when applied two to three times daily as leave-on topical products. A therapeutic shampoo, which by nature is a wash-off product, would be expected to be used once daily while the scalp disorder was in an acute stage; twice weekly for chronic conditions; and once weekly for a maintenance regime for individuals particularly susceptible to seborrhoeic dermatitis or dandruff. As miconazole is considered safe for use in more rigorous regimes than that given for miconazole shampoos, it would follow that the latter is considered safe for use.


The absorption of miconazole is not significant when applied to the skin or mucous membranes. Oral human administration indicates that miconazole has low bioavailability, plasma and urine levels are low and most of the drug can be recovered unmetabolised from the faeces.
