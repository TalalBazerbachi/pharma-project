Composition:

Each film coated tablet contains: 10 mg Hyoscine – N – butylbromide.

 

Clinical particulars:

Therapeutic indications:

Nospasm is indicated for the relief of spasm of the genito-urinary tract or gastro- intestinal tract and for the symptomatic relief of irritable bowel syndrome.


Pharmacological properties:

Pharmacodynamic properties

Nospasm exerts a spasmolytic action on the smooth muscle of the gastrointestinal, biliary and genito-urinary tracts. Peripheral anticholinergic action results from ganglion-muscarinic activity.

 

Pharmacokinetic properties:

Following oral administration, hyoscine-N-butylbromide concentrates in the tissue of the gastrointestinal tract, liver and kidneys. As a quaternary ammonium derivative, hyoscine-N-butylbromide does not pass the blood – brain – barrier or enter the central nervous system.

 

Contraindications:

Nospasm should not be administered to patient with myasthenia gravis , megacolon and narrow angle glaucoma. In addition , they should not given to patient with known hypersensitivity to hyoscine-N butylbromide or any other component of the product.

 

Special warnings and special precautions for use:

 

Nospasm should be used with caution in conditions characterized by tachycardia such as thyrotoxicosis , cardiac insufficiency or failure and in cardiac surgery where it may further accelerate the heart rate. Due to the risk of anticholinergic complications, caution should be used in patient susceptible to intestinal or urinary outlet obstructions.

 

Because of the possibility that anticholinergics my reduce sweating , Nospasm should administered with cation to patient with pyrexia.

Elevation of intraocular pressure may be produced by the administration of anticholinergic agents in patient with undiagnosed and therefore untreated narrow angle glaucoma.

Therefor patient should seek urgent ophthalmological advice in case they should develop a painful , red eye with loss of vision whilst or after taking anticholinergic agents.

Consult your doctor if you have developed new symptom , or if your symptoms worsen, or if they not improve after 2 weeks of treatment.

 

Interaction with other medical products and other forms of interaction:

The anticholinergic effect of drugs such as tricyclic anti- depressants, Antihistamine , quinidine, amantadine , pheno-thoazines, disopyramide and other anticholinergics ( e.g. tiotropium , ipratropium)  may be intensified by Nospasm .

Concomitant treatment with dopamine antagonists such as metoclopramide may result in diminution of the effects of both drugs on the gastrointestinal tract.

 

The tachycardic effects of beta-adrenergic agents may enhanced by Nospasm.

Pregnancy and lactation:

There is no definite evidence of ill consequence during human pregnancy; anima studies have shown hazard.

Medicines should not be used in pregnancy , especially during the first trimester , unless the expected benefit is thought to outweigh any possible risk.

Safety during lactation has not been established


Posology and method of administration:

Adults: 2 tablets four times daily . For the symptomatic relief of irritable bowel syndrome, the recommended starting dose is 1 tablet three times daily, this can be increased up to 2 tablets four times daily if necessary.

Children 6-12 years: 1 tablet three times daily.

Nospasm should be swallowed whole with adequate water


Many of the undesirable effects can be assigned to the anticholinergic properties of Nospasm

Uncommon: Skin reactions , tachycardia , dry mouth, dyshidrosis

Rare: Urinary retention, hypersensitivity

 

Overdose:

 

Serious signs of poisoning following acute overdosage have not been observed in man. In the case of overdosage, anticholinergic effects such as urinary retention , dry moth , reddening of the skin, tachycardia, inhibition of gastrointestinal motility and transit visual disturbances may occur.

Therapy: Gastric lavage should followed by magnesium sulphate (15%). Symptoms of Nospasm overdosage respond to parasympathomimetic.  For patients with glaucoma, pilocarpine should be given locally. catheterization may be required for urinary retention.


Store below 30 C

التركيب:

يحتوي كل قرص مغلف بطبقة رقيقة على 10 ملجم هيوسين -ن-بيوتايل بروماير.

الخصائص السريريه:

دواعي الاستعمال العلاجية:

يستعمل نوسبازم لازالة التقلصات العضليه في السبيل التناسلي البولي, السبيل الهضمي , ولعلاج الاعراض المصاحبة للقولون العصبيز

 

الخواص الدوائيه:

خواص التاثير الدوائي:

يعمل نوسبازم كمزيل للتقلصات وذلك عن طريق تاثيره على العضلات الملساء في السبيل الهضمي والمراري والتناسلي البولي. ويرجع مفعول نوسبازم كمضاد طرفي لعمل الاسيتايل كولين الى تثبيط العقد العصبيه الموجوده في الحشى وتأثيره كمضاد للمسكارين.

 

خواص حركيه الدواء:

عند تناول هيوسين -ن-بيوتايل بروماير عن طريق الفم, يتركز بصورة اساسية في السبيل الهضمي, الكبد والكلى. ولان هيوسين -ن-بيوتايل بروماير هو مشتق من امونيومي رباعي فانه لا ينفذ من خلال الحاجز الدموي المخي وبالتالي لا ينفذ داخل الجهاز العصبي المركزي.

 

موانع الاستعمال :

لا يستعمل نوسبازم مع مرضى مايثينيا جرافيس , تضخم القولون , الجلوكوما ( زرق) ضيقة الزاوية. يمنع ايضا استخدام نوسبازم مع المرضى الذين يعانون من الحساسية تجاه هيوسين -ن-بيوتايل بروماير او اي مكونات هذا المستحضر.

 

تحذيرات خاصه واحتياطات خاصه بالنسبة للاستعمال:

يجب الحذر عند استعمال نوسبازم في الحالات التي يحدث فيها اسراع ضربات القلب مثل حالات التسمم الدرقي, قصور او فشل القلب الوظيفي , وفي جراحات القلب حيث انه قد يعزز من اسراع ضربات القلب. نظرا لخطورة حدوث مضاعفات جراء التاثير المضاد لعمل الاستايل كولين , فانه يجب  الحذر عند استعماله في المرضى المعرضين لحدوث انسداد معوي او بولي.

نظرا لان مضادات عمل الاسيتايل كولين قد تؤدي الى تثبيط افراز العرق فانه يجب الحذر عند استعمال نوسبازم في المرضى الذين يعانون من الحمى.

قد يؤدي استعمال مضادات عمل الاستايل كولين الى ارتفاع الضغط داخل مقلة العين وذلك في المرضى الذين يعانون من الجلوكوما ضيقة الزاويه ولم يتم تشخيص المرض لديهم وبالتالي لا يتم معالجتهم. وعليه يجب على المرضى الاسراع باستشاره طبيب العيون في حالة حدوث الم واحمرار في العين مع فقد الرؤيه بعد تناول مضادات عمل الاسيتايل كولين.

يجب استشارة الطبيب في حالة ظهور اعراض جديده, او اذا زادت الاعراض سوءا او في حالة عدم تحسن الاعراض خلال اسبوعين من العلاج.

 

التفاعل مع الادويه الاخرى والانواع الاخرى من التفاعل:

قد يعزز نوسبازم من التاثير المضاد لعمل الاسيتايل كولين لدى بعض العلاجات مثل مضادات الاكتئاب ذات الحلقه الثلاثيه, مضادات الهيستامين , الكوينيدين , امانتادين , فينوثيازينز, ديسوبيراميد ومضادات عمل الاسيتايل كولين الاخرى مثل تيوتروبيوم وابراتروبيوم.

ان التناول المتزامن لنوسبازم مع مضادات الدوبامين مثل متوكلوبراميد قد يؤدي الى قلة كفائة كلا المستحضرين على السبيل الهضمي. وقد يعزز نوسبازم من اسراع ضربات القلب الناتج عن تناول مستحضرات من نوعيه مثبطات مستقبلات بيتا

Symptoms: Serious signs of poisoning following acute overdosage have not been observed in man. In the case of overdosage, anticholinergic effects such as urinary retention, dry mouth, reddening of the skin, tachycardia, inhibition of gastrointestinal motility and transient visual disturbances may occur, and Cheynes-Stokes respiration has been reported. Therapy: In the case of oral poisoning, gastric lavage with medicinal charcoal should be followed by magnesium sulfate (15%). Symptoms of Nospasm overdosage respond to parasympathomimetics. For patients with glaucoma, pilocarpine should be given locally. Cardiovascular complications should be treated according to usual therapeutic principles. In case of respiratory paralysis, intubation and artificial respiration. Catheterisation may be required for urinary retention. In addition, appropriate supportive measures should be administered as required.


ATC code: A03BB01 Nospasm exerts a spasmolytic action on the smooth muscle of the gastrointestinal, biliary and genito-urinary tracts. As a quaternary ammonium derivative, Hyoscine-N-Butylbromide does not enter the central nervous system. Therefore, anticholinergic side effects at the central nervous system do not occur. Peripheral anticholinergic action results from a ganglionblocking action within the visceral wall as well as from an anti-muscarinic activity.


Absorption As a quaternary ammonium compound, Hyoscine-N-Butylbromide is highly polar and hence only partially absorbed following oral (8%) or rectal (3%) administration. After oral administration of single doses of Hyoscine-N-Butylbromide in the range of 20 to 400 mg, mean peak plasma concentrations between 0.11 ng/mL and 2.04 ng/mL were found at approximately 2 hours. In the same dose range, the observed mean AUC0-tz-values varied from 0.37 to 10.7 ng h/mL. The median absolute bioavailabilities of different dosage forms, i.e. Film-Coated Tablets, suppositories and oral solution, containing 100 mg of Hyoscine-NButylbromide each were found to be less than 1%. Distribution Because of its high affinity for muscarinic receptors and nicotinic receptors, Hyoscine-NButylbromide is mainly distributed on muscle cells of the abdominal and pelvic area as well as in the intramural ganglia of the abdominal organs. Plasma protein binding (albumin) of Hyoscine-N-Butylbromide is approximately 4.4%. Animal studies demonstrate that HyoscineN-Butylbromide does not pass the blood-brain barrier, but no clinical data to this effect is available. Hyoscine-N-Butylbromide (1 mM) has been observed to interact with the choline transport (1.4 nM) in epithelial cells of human placenta in vitro. Metabolism and elimination Following oral administration of single doses in the range of 100 to 400 mg, the terminal elimination half-lives ranged from 6.2 to 10.6 hours. The main metabolic pathway is the hydrolytic cleavage of the ester bond. Orally administered Hyoscine-N-Butylbromide is excreted in the faeces and in the urine. Studies in man show that 2 to 5% of radioactive doses is eliminated renally after oral, and 0.7 to 1.6% after rectal administration. Approximately 90% of recovered radioactivity can be found in the faeces after oral administration. The urinary excretion of Hyoscine-N-Butylbromide is less than 0.1% of the dose. The mean apparent oral clearances after oral doses of 100 to 400 mg range from 881 to 1420 L/min, whereas the corresponding volumes of distribution for the same range vary from 6.13 to 11.3 x 105 L, probably due to very low systemic availability. The metabolites excreted via the renal route bind poorly to the muscarinic receptors and are therefore not considered to contribute to the effect of the Hyoscine-N-Butylbromide.


In limited reproductive toxicity studies Hyoscine-N-Butylbromide showed no evidence of teratogenicity in rats at 200 mg/kg in the diet or in rabbits at 200 mg/kg by oral gavage or 50 mg/kg by subcutaneous injection. Fertility in the rat was not impaired at doses of up to 200 mg/kg in the diet.
