  SFDA PIL (Patient Information Leaflet (PIL) are under review by Saudi Food and Drug Authority)

Secnid® Tablets contains Secnidazole 500 mg.

It is indicated in:

- Intestinal and hepatic amoebiasis.

- Trichomal infections of urogenital tract.


Do not use Secnid® Tablets and tell your doctor or pharmacist if you have:

- Hypersensitivity to any component in the drug.

- In cases of epilepsy or organic CNS disease.

 

Take special care with Secnid® Tablets

- Secnid® should be used with great care in patients with blood dyscrasias or with active disease of the central nervous system.

- Precautions must be taken if SECNID is taken concurrently with alcohol or drugs formulated with alcohol as it may provoke a disulfiram like reaction in some individuals.

 

Using other medicines

Concurrent use of Secnid® with disulfiram may cause acute psychosis or confusion.

 

Pregnancy and breast-feedingConcurrent

use of Secnid® with disulfiram may cause acute psychosis or confusion.

 

Driving and using machines

Not known.


Adults:

- Acute intestinal amoebiasis:

4 tablets taken as a single dose for one day.

- Hepatic amoebiasis:

3 tablets taken as single dose for 5 days.

- Urogenital trichomoniasis:

4 tablets taken as a single dose for one day.

 

Children:

- Acute intestinal amoebiasis:

30 mg / kg body weight as a single or several divided doses for one day.

- Hepatic amebiasis:

30 mg/ kg body weight as a single dose or several divided doses for 5 days.


It may cause in few cases gastro-intestinal disturbances especially nausea and vomiting. It may cause headache. Anorexia or rarely Pseudo membranous colitis (PMC).


- Store at temperature not exceeding 30 °C.

- Keep out of the reach of children.

- Do not use Secnid® Tabletsafter the expiry date (EXP) which is stated on the box and blister.

Medicines should not be disposed of via wastewater or household waste. Ask your pharmacist how to dispose of medicines no longer required. These measures will help to protect the environment.


The active substance is: Secnidazole. Each tablet contains Secnidazole 500 mg.

The other ingredients are: microcrystalline cellulose, Maize starch, sodium starch glycollate, Colloidal silicon dioxide and Magnesium stearate.


Secnid® tablets present in carton box containing blister containing 4 tablets along with a leaflet. Secnid® tablets is white flat circular uncoated tablets.

Secnidazole is a 5-nitroinlidawle derivative. It has antiamoebiasis and antitricomoniasis action. It has also a bactericidal activity or antibacterial activity against many obligate anaerobic bacteria such as Bactericides’ and Clostridium species. It has also activity against the facultative anaerobes
Gardnella vaginalis and Helicobacter pylori and against some Spirochetes.


Secnidazole is absorbed following oral administration. It reaches the blood with a half life of about 20 hours. It crosses the placenta and it is excreted in urine and to a small extent in nursing milk. It is metabolized in liver.
