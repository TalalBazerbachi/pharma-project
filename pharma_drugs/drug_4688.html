Eldoquin forte Cream contains the
active substance Hydroquinone.
This is used for reducing
hyperpigmentation of skin.
Hyperpigmentation refers to brown
skin spots which are caused by
an increase in the amount of
melanin pigment in the skin. It can
be hereditary, as in freckles, or
develop with increased age, as in
“old age spots”. It can be related
to pregnancy or from the use of
oral contraceptives, as in melasma
(also called the mask of pregnancy).
Hyperpigmentation usually develops
gradually and becomes markedly
more apparent after sun exposure
and tanning. Hyperpigmented skin
can be lightened by using a product
containing hydroquinone as its skin
bleaching agent. Hydroquinone
works by slowing down the
production of melanin pigment in
the skin. As less and less melanin
is produced the darkened skin
gradually fades to match normal
skin.
Therapeutic Indications:
Eldoquin forte Cream is indicated
for the gradual bleaching of
hyperpigmented skin conditions
such as chloasma, melasma,
freckles, senile lentigines and
other unwanted areas of melanin
hyperpigmentation. It is intended for
night-time use only since it contains
no sunblocking agents.


Do not take Eldoquin forte Cream
if you have hypersensitivity to any
of the ingredients (as mentioned in
section 6). Test for skin sensitivity
before using Eldoquin forte Cream
Cream by applying a small amount
to an unbroken patch of skin and
check in 24 hours. Minor redness
is not a contraindication, but where
there is itching or vesicle formation

or excessive inflammatory response,
further treatment is not advised.
Taking other medicines, herbal
or dietary supplements: Please
tell your doctor or pharmacist if you
are taking or have recently taken any
other medicines, including medicines
obtained without a prescription.
Taking Eldoquin forte Cream
with food and drink: No reports
available, but for more than 25 years

Mechanism of action:
Eldoquin forte 4% Cream contains the active substance Hydroquinone. This is used for reducing hyperpigmentation of skin. Hyperpigmentation refers to brown skin spots which are caused by an increase in the amount of melanin pigment in the skin. It can be hereditary, as in freckles, or develop with increased age, as in “old age spots”. It can be related to pregnancy or from the use of oral contraceptives, as in melasma (also called the mask of pregnancy). Hyperpigmentation usually develops gradually and becomes markedly more apparent after sun exposure and tanning. Hyperpigmented skin can be lightened by using a product containing hydroquinone as its skin bleaching agent. Hydroquinone works by slowing down the production of melanin pigment in the skin. As less and less melanin is produced the darkened skin gradually fades to match normal skin.
