Nizoral® 2% shampoo is a medicated shampoo that contains the active substance ketoconazole which belongs to a group of medicines called antifungals.
Like other shampoos, it also contains ingredients to clean your hair.
It is indicated in the prevention and treatment of infections of the scalp and skin associated with fungal infections such as:
• Pityriasis versicolor (Tinea versicolor); a fungal infection of the skin causing white to brown patches on body parts
• Seborrheic dermatitis; a dry or greasy scaling of the scalp and other parts of the body.
• Pityriasis capitis; also known as dandruff.
These conditions can be itchy. They are caused by a fungus (yeast) called ‘Malassezia’. This fungus is found on everyone’s skin, but sometimes it can overgrow and causes problems. Nizoral® 2% shampoo works by killing the fungus and stopping it from coming back.


Do not use Nizoral® 2% shampoo if:
You are allergic to the active ingredient or any of the other ingredients listed in section 6.
Warnings and precautions
Take a special care with Nizoral® 2% shampoo if
• If you use a lotion or cream on your hair that contains a steroid, such as betamethasone or hydrocortisone:
• You should use less and less of the lotion or cream during the first 2 to 3 weeks of Nizoral shampoo treatment
• This will stop your condition flaring up while Nizoral shampoo starts to work.
If you have any question about stopping your steroid treatment, ask your doctor or pharmacist.
Children and adolescents
Nizoral Shampoo is not recommended for use in infants and children.
Other medicines and Nizoral shampoo
Nizoral® 2% shampoo does not normally react with other medicines. However, tell your doctor or pharmacist
if you are taking or have recently taken any other medicines. This includes medicines that you buy without a prescription or herbal medicines.
Pregnancy and breastfeeding
Nizoral® shampoo can be used if you are pregnant or breast-feeding.
Ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking any medicine if you are pregnant or breast-feeding.
Driving and using machines
Nizoral® 2% shampoo is not likely to affect you being able to drive or use any tools or machines.


Always use this medicine exactly as your doctor has told you. Nizoral® shampoo is for use on your hair or skin only. Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you are not sure.
How often you should use Nizoral shampoo
The recommended dose is:
Usually one palmful of the shampoo is enough for one wash.
Treatment
• pityriasis versicolor; once daily for 1–5 days.
• seborrheic dermatitis and pityriasis capitis; twice weekly for 2–4 weeks
If your scalp has not cleared within 4 weeks, consult your doctor or pharmacist.
Prophylaxis
• pityriasis versicolor; once daily for a maximum of 3 days during a single treatment course before the summer, as patches of pityriasis versicolor become more apparent on exposure to sun.
• seborrheic dermatitis and pityriasis capitis; once every 1–2 weeks to stop the symptoms coming back. If you stop using Nizoral® 2% shampoo altogether, your symptoms may become a problem again.
Using Nizoral® 2% shampoo
• Wash your hair with the scalp or infected part of skin with the shampoo.
• Leave it on for 3 to 5 minutes
• Rinse thoroughly
If Nizoral shampoo gets in your eyes Gently bathe your eyes in cold water.
Using your normal shampoo
• If you need to wash your hair between treatments, use a normal shampoo.
• If your hair is very thick or long, you can wash it with your usual shampoo and then use Nizoral® 2% shampoo.
If you swallow Nizoral shampoo
If you swallow any shampoo, tell your doctor straight away.
If you forget to use Nizoral® 2% shampoo
• If you miss a treatment with Nizoral shampoo, you should use it as soon as you remember
• Do not use the shampoo twice to make up for a missed treatment
If you have any further questions on the use of this product, ask your doctor or pharmacist


Like all medicines, this medicine can cause side effects, although not everybody gets them.
Stop using Nizoral® 2% shampoo and tell your doctor straight away if you notice or suspect any of the following. You may need urgent medical treatment.
• Allergic reaction, swelling of the face, lips, mouth, tongue, or throat which may cause difficulty in swallowing or breathing. This can affect fewer than 1 in 1000 people
• Severe irritation or reddening of the skin where the shampoo has been used, or other signs of allergy during the first few days of treatment. This can affect fewer than 1 in 100 people
• Peeling or blistering of the skin. This can affect fewer than 1 in 1000 people

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you notice or suspect any of the following side effects:
Uncommon side effects (affects fewer than 1 in 100 people)
• Infection of the hair roots
• Increase in production of tears
• Unusual hair loss or thinning
• Dry skin, rash, burning feeling
• Changes in how your hair feels
Rare side effects (affects fewer than 1 in 1000 people)
• Taste impairment
• Irritation of the eyes
• Acne
• Flaking skin
Side effects with unknown frequencies (cannot be estimated from the available literature)
• Hives (also known as nettle rash or urticaria)
• Hair colour changes

Nizoral Shampoo

1 ml cutaneous and scalp solution contains 20 mg ketoconazole. For the full list of excipients, see section 6.1.

Shampoo for cutaneous use, including the scalp. Reddish, clear, viscous solution

For the treatment of seborrhoeic dermatitis and pityriasis versicolor.

 

Nizoral is intended for use in adolescents and adults.


The required amount of Nizoral is spread onto moistened hair or over the moistened, affected parts of the body. The shampoo is briefly massaged in and should be left on for 3‑5 minutes to take effect. The shampoo is then rinsed out/off with plenty of warm water.

Prior or subsequent hair washing with a commercial shampoo is not required. The Nizoral formulation allows simultaneous cleansing of the hair.

Pregnancy

There are no or limited amount of data from the use of ketoconazole in pregnant women. After topical application of NizoralÒ to the scalp of non-pregnant volunteers, no ketoconazole is measurable in plasma. After topical application of NizoralÒ over the whole body, ketoconazole is measurable in plasma.

Ketoconazole crosses the placenta. In animal studies, foetal damage has occurred after systemic use of ketoconazole (see section 5.3).

There are no known risks associated with the use of NizoralÒ during pregnancy. Hence, NizoralÒ may be used during pregnancy after an appropriate benefit/risk assessment.

 

Breast-feeding

There are no or limited amount of data from the use of ketoconazole during breast-feeding. After topical application of NizoralÒ to the scalp of non-breast-feeding volunteers, no ketoconazole is measurable in plasma. After topical application of NizoralÒ over the whole body, ketoconazole is measurable in plasma.

Ketoconazole is excreted in human milk. There are no known risks associated with the use of NizoralÒ during breast-feeding. Hence, NizoralÒ may be used during breast-feeding after an appropriate benefit/risk assessment.

While breast-feeding, NizoralnÒ should not be applied in the breast area. This will prevent contact between the infant and treated parts of the body.

Pharmacotherapeutic group: antifungals for topical use, imidazole and triazole derivatives

ATC code: D01AC08

 

Ketoconazole, a synthetic imidazole dioxolane derivative, is a potent inhibitor of the biosynthesis of ergosterol, a major sterol of the cell membrane of yeast and other fungi. Ergosterol is an important regulator of membrane permeability. At fungistatic concentrations, proliferation of the plasmalemma and thickening of the cell wall occurs. These morphological changes are accompanied by changes in membrane permeability, which may lead to selective inhibition in the uptake of essential nutritive substances. At fungicidal concentrations, necrosis of the cell interior occurs.

Pharmacological tests show that ketoconazole acts against a variety of pathogens (especially dermatophytes, e.g. Trichophyton spp., Epidermophyton spp., Microsporum spp., and yeasts, such as Candida spp. and Malassezia spp. (Pityrosporum spp.). Nizoral rapidly relieves the symptoms of desquamation and pruritus which are generally associated with seborrheic dermatitis, Pityriasis capitis (dandruff) and Pityriasis versicolor.


Plasma concentrations of ketoconazole were not detectable after topical application of Nizoral to the scalp. Plasma concentrations of ketoconazole were detectable after topical application of Nizoral over the entire body surface area.


 

Subacute dermal toxicity

In studies on the subacute dermal toxicity of ketoconazole in rabbits, barely visible signs of irritation occurred in the placebo group and in the active treatment groups.

 

Chronic toxicity

In chronic toxicity studies following systemic administration, ketoconazole showed hepatotoxic effects in dogs. In rats, pathological changes in the kidneys, adrenal cortex and ovaries were observed, as well as increased bone fragility in the female rats.

 

Mutagenic and tumorigenic potential

There were no indications of any mutagenic or carcinogenic properties for ketoconazole.

 

Toxicity to reproduction

Systemically administered, ketoconazole impairs fertility and embryo development in rats and leads to malformations of the skeleton and cardiovascular system.
