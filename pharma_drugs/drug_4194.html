  SFDA PIL (Patient Information Leaflet (PIL) are under review by Saudi Food and Drug Authority)

Kapect is a palatable flavoured suspension containing kaolin, a hydrated aluminum silicate, and pectin, a purified carbohydrate product from citrus fruit rind. Kapect is a simple, safe and efficient antidiarrhoeal product which combines adsorbent, protectant and demulcent properties essential for the treatment of diarrhoea.

Kapect is used for:

- Symptomatic treatment of mild to moderately acute diarrhea in children and adults.

- As an adjuvant to rehydration therapy in treatment of diarrhea.


Do not take Kapect if:

-You are allergic (hypersensitive) to kaolin, pectin or to any of the other ingredients in Kapect (see Section 6: Further Information).

- You have fever.

- You have pseudomembranous colitis.

- You have obstructive bowel lesion.

Do not take this medicine if any of the above apply to you. If you are not sure, talk to your doctor or pharmacist before taking Kapect.

Take a special care if:

- You are elderly.

- You are going to give it to children younger than 3 years.

Taking other medicines:

Please tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking or have recently taken any other medicines. This includes medicines you buy without a prescription, particulary the following drugs: Tetracycline, chloroquine, digoxin, clindamycin, lincomycin, hydroxychloroquine, aspirin, phenothiazines, lovastin.

Pregnancy and breast-feeding:

Talk to your doctor or pharmacist before taking this medicine if:

- You are pregnant, might become pregnant, or think you may be pregnant.

- You are breast-feeding or planning to breast-feed.


Adults: 1 tablespoonful (15 ml) 4 – 8 times daily, or as directed by the physician.

Children 3 – 5 years: 1 teaspoonful (5 ml) 3 – 6 times daily, or as directed by the physician.

Children from 6 – 11 years: 1 tablespoonful (15 ml) 2 – 4 times daily, or as directed by the physician.

Children 12 years or older: 1 tablespoonful (15 ml) 4 times daily, or as directed by the physician.

If you take more Kapect than you should:

Talk to your doctor or go to a hospital straight away. Remember to take any medicine that is left with you so the doctor knows what you have taken.


Like all medicines, Kapect can cause side effects, although not everybody gets them.

The side effects are usually mild and transient, and it may include constipation.


Keep out of the reach and sight of children.

Store at a temperature not exceeding 30°C.

Store in the original package, keep the bottle tightly closed.

Use Kapect before the expiry date shown on the label.

Medicines should not be disposed of via wastewater or household waste.

Ask your pharmacist how to dispose medicines no longer required. These measures will help to protect the environment.

Kapect is a palatable flavoured suspension containing kaolin, a hydrated aluminum silicate, and pectin, a purified carbohydrate product from citrus fruit rind. Kaolin acts by adsorbing gases, toxins and bacteria from the alimentary tract and by increasing the bulk of stools. Pectin not only provides additional adsorptive power to that of kaolin, but it also has a coating effect on the bowel. Thus, Kapect is a simple, safe and efficient antidiarrhoeal product which combines adsorbent, protectant and demulcent properties essential for the treatment of diarrhoea.
