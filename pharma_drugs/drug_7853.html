DL-alpha-tocopheryl acetate is the active
ingredient of Vital E 400™ , is a man-made
form of vitamin E. Although the exact role of
vitamin E has not yet been worked out, it is
known to play an important part in metabolismthe
chemical reactions that are taking place in
the body all the time.
Vital E 400™ is used in certain illnesses which
prevent the body from absorbing enough vitamin
E from the diet.
By taking extra vitamin E in the form of
Vital E 400™, the body gets all it needs. The
medical names for these illnesses are cystic
fibrosis (an inherited disorder affecting the
lungs, pancreas and other glands), chronic
cholestasis
(a disease of the liver) and abetalipoproteinaemia
(a rare inherited disease which causes a
deficiency of fat in the blood and severely
affects growth).


Do not take Vital E 400™
- If you are allergic to vitamin E or any of other
ingredients in the capsules.
- If you are allergic to soya, as this product
contains Soybean oil.
- If you have hereditary problems of fructose
intolerance, glucose-galactose malabsorption or
sucrase-isomaltase insufficiency.
Take special care with Vital E 400™
- If you are taking an oral contraceptive pill.
- If you have ever had a thrombosis (a clot inside
a blood vessel).
Vitamin E has been reported to increase the risk
of thrombosis in patients predisposed to this
condition, including patients taking oestrogens.
This finding has not been confirmed but should
be borne in mind, in particular women taking
oral contraceptives containing oestrogens.
Precautions
This formulation is not adequate for children.
This category of patients will use pharmaceutical
forms and age-appropriate concentrations.
Vital E 400™ should not be administered
concomitantly with other drugs containing
vitamin E, unless the total dose of vitamin E
administered does not exceed the maximum
recommended dose due to the risk of
hypervitaminosis.
Taking or using other medicines
Please tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are
taking or have recently taken any other
medicines, including the oral contraceptive pill
and any medicines obtained without a
prescription.
Pregnancy and breast feeding
Ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice before
taking any medicine if you are pregnant or think
you may be, or are planning to become pregnant,
or are breast-feeding. The effects of high doses
of vitamin E during pregnancy and breast
feeding are not known. Consequently, Vital E
400™ is not recommended during pregnancy
and breast feeding. 

Driving and using machines
Vital E 400™ should not affect your ability to
drive or use machinery.
Important information about some of the
ingredients of Vital E 400™
This product contains Soybean oil, do not take
it if you are allergic to soya.


Take 1-2 softgel capsule daily with food or as
prescribed by your doctor.

If you take more Vital E 400™ than you
should
If you or anyone else accidentally swallows a
large amount of Vitamin E Capsules, do not
panic as it is unlikely to cause any serious
harm but may experience a stomach upset.
Obtain medical advice from your doctor or
pharmacist immediately.
If you forget to take Vital E 400™
Vital E 400™ should be taken every day but it
is not important to take it at any particular
time. There is no need to take extra the
following day if you forget to take a dose.


Like all medicines Vital E 400™ can
sometimes cause side effects, although not
everyone gets them and they are very unlikely
with this medicine.
It is possible that you might develop diarrhoea
or a stomach upset.

Vital E 400 mg Softgel Capsules

Each softgel capsule contains: Vitamin E (dl-alpha-tocopheryl acetate) 400 mg. For a full list of excipients, see section 6.1

Soft Gelatin Capsules. JP Vitamin E 400mg capsules are clear transparent, oval shaped, soft gelatin capsules, filled with pale yellow coloured oily liquid.

Vitamin E Supplement
dl-alpha-tocopheryl acetate is the active ingredient of JP Vitamin E 400mg, is a man-made form of
vitamin E. Although the exact role of vitamin E has not yet been worked out, it is known to play an
important part in metabolism-the chemical reactions that are taking place in the body all the time.
JP Vitamin E 400mg is used in certain illnesses which prevent the body from absorbing enough
vitamin E from the diet.
By taking extra vitamin E in the form of JP Vitamin E 400mg softgel capsule, the body gets all it needs.
The medical names for these illnesses are cystic fibrosis (an inherited disorder affecting the lungs,
pancreas and other glands), chronic cholestasis (a disease of the liver) and abetalipoproteinaemia (a rare
inherited disease which causes a deficiency of fat in the blood and severely affects growth).


Take 1-2 softgel capsules daily with food or as prescribed by your doctor.


Do not take if you are allergic to vitamin E or any of other ingredients in the capsules and if you are allergic to soya, as this product contains Soybean oil. Patients with rare hereditary problems of fructose intolerance, glucose-galactose malabsorption or sucrase-isomaltase insufficiency should not take this medicine.

Consult your doctor before taking if:
• If you are taking an oral contraceptive pill.
• If you have ever had a thrombosis (a clot inside a blood vessel).
− Vitamin E has been reported to increase the risk of thrombosis in patients predisposed to this
condition, including patients taking oestrogens. This finding has not been confirmed but should be
borne in mind when selecting patients for treatment, in particular women taking oral contraceptives
containing oestrogens.
− A higher incidence of necrotising enterocolitis has been noted in lower weight premature infants
(less than 1.5kg) treated with vitamin E.


Vitamin E 400mg should not be administered concomitantly with other drugs containing
Vitamin E, unless the total dose of vitamin E administered does not exceed the maximum
recommended dose due to the risk of hypervitaminosis. Please tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are
taking or have recently taken any other medicines, including the oral contraceptive pill and any
medicines obtained without a prescription.
Vitamin E may increase the risk of thrombosis in patients taking oestrogens.


Pregnancy Category C:
If you are pregnant or breast-feeding, think you may be pregnant or are planning to have a baby,
ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking Vitamin E 400 mg. The effects of high
doses of vitamin E during pregnancy and breast feeding are not known.
Consequently, JP Vitamin E 400mg is not recommended during pregnancy and breast feeding. You
should not take this product if you are pregnant or breast-feeding; unless your doctor decides it is
absolutely necessary.


Vitamin E 400 mg is not likely to affect you being able to drive or use any tools or machines


Side effects are rare. In case of any side effects, contact your pharmacist or physician or to the

The exact role of vitamin E in the animal organism has not yet been established. Vitamin E is known
to exert an important physiological function as an antioxidant for fats, with a sparing action on
Vitamin A, carotenoids and on unsaturated fatty acids. Other work has demonstrated that Vitamin E
is connected with the maintenance of certain factors essential for the normal metabolic cycle.


Vitamin E is absorbed from the gastrointestinal tract. Most of the vitamin appears in the lymph and is
then widely distributed to all tissues. Most of the dose is slowly excreted in the bile and the
remainder is eliminated in the urine as glucuronides of tocopheronic acid or other metabolites.


No harmful effects on humans are to be expected when used as recommended.
