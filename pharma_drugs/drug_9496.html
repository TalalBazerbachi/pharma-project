1.What Xylocaine is and what it is used for

 Xylocaine ointment contains a local anaesthetic agent and has a pain-relieving effect. Xylocaine ointment does not disrupt wound healing.

Xylocaine ointment is used for pain relief associated with e.g. insect bites, wounds, burn injuries, ulcerated nipples during breast-feeding as well as in preparation for wound cleaning.

Xylocaine ointment is also used for pain relief in various anal conditions (e.g. haemorrhoids, anal fissures and painful bowel evacuations).

Xylocaine ointment can be used without a prescription in adults and children aged over 18 months. Prescription by a doctor is required for use in children aged less than 18 months.

Lidocaine, which is an ingredient of Xylocaine, may also be approved for the treatment of other conditions not named in this leaflet. Ask your doctor, pharmacist or other healthcare staff if you have any other questions, and always follow their advice.


2.What you need to know before you use Xylocaine

Do not use Xylocaine

- if you are allergic (hypersensitive) to lidocaine or any other local anaesthetic or to any of the other ingredients in Xylocaine (listed in section 6).

Warnings and precautions

- In the case of burn injuries, Xylocaine ointment should not be used on areas larger than the palm of the hand.

- Xylocaine ointment must not be used on children aged less than 18 months unless prescribed by a doctor.

- Talk to your doctor before using Xylocaine ointment if you suffer from disturbances to the heart’s electrical conduction system (heart block), severe liver disease or severe kidney impairment, or have poor general health.

Other medicines and Xylocaine

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking, have recently taken or might take any other medicines.

The risk of side effects increases if you use Xylocaine ointment at the same time as certain other medicines, particularly

· other local anaesthetics

· medicines for irregular heart activity (so-called antiarrhythmics).

Pregnancy and breast-feeding

Ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking any medicine.

Temporary use of Xylocaine ointment during pregnancy is unlikely to have any harmful effects on the foetus.

Lidocaine is excreted into human milk, but is unlikely to affect the breastfed child. However, consult your doctor in cases of more than temporary use of Xylocaine during breast-feeding.

Driving and using machines

When used at recommended doses, Xylocaine will not affect your ability to drive a car or use machines.

Xylocainee ointment contains propylene glycol

Xylocainee ointment contains propylene glycol, which can cause skin irritation.


3.How to use Xylocaine

 For painful wounds or burn injuries: Apply the ointment directly to the wound or burn injury. The ointment can also be applied to a wound dressing which is then applied to the injured area. Maximal pain relief is achieved after approx. 5 minutes. If it feels sticky, the ointment can then be wiped off or rinsed off with water.

Prior to wound cleaning: follow the same procedure. The cleaning can commence approx. 5 minutes after the ointment has been applied to the wound.

Ulcerated nipples in connection with breast-feeding: Apply the ointment 5–10 minutes prior to breast-feeding and thoroughly wipe or rinse off with water before the actual breast-feeding.

For anal conditions: Apply the ointment to irritated areas 1–3 times daily. In the case of painful bowel motions, the ointment should be applied 5–10 minutes prior to using the toilet.

If the anal condition does not subside after 2–3 weeks or if anal bleeding occurs, contact your doctor.

 Other dosages as prescribed by your doctor.

For hygienic reasons, do not use the same tube for anal conditions and other wound treatment.

If you use more Xylocaine than you should

If you have taken too much medicine you should always contact a doctor, hospital or Poisons Information Centre (tel. 112) to get advice and an assessment of the risk.

If you have any further questions on the use of this medicine, ask your doctor or pharmacist.


4. Possible side effects

 Like all medicines, Xylocaine can cause side effects, although not everybody gets them.

Rare (less than 1 in 1,000 persons are affected): allergic reactions; in the most severe cases: anaphylactic shock (severe allergic reaction with e.g. skin rash, swelling, fever, difficulty breathing and fainting). If you experience severe allergic reactions such as this you should immediately seek help at a hospital Emergency Department.


5.How to store Xylocaine

 The ointment should be stored at room temperature (maximum 25°C) to maintain its soft and smooth consistency.

Keep this medicine out of the sight and reach of children.

Do not use this medicine after the expiry date which is stated on the packaging. The expiry date refers to the last day of that month.

Do not throw away any medicines via wastewater or household waste. Ask your pharmacist how to throw away medicines you no longer use. These measures will help protect the environment.

1. ما هو زيلوكايين وما هي دواعي استخدامه

يحتوي مرهم زيلوكايين على عامل تخدير موضعي وله مفعول مسكن للآلام. لا يعيق مرهم زيلوكايين شفاء الجروح.

يُستخدم مرهم زيلوكايين في تسكين الآلام الناجمة عن، على سبيل المثال، لدغات الحشرات، والجروح، وإصابات الحروق، وتقرح الحلمات أثناء عملية الرضاعة الطبيعية وكذلك في التحضير لتنظيف الجروح.

كما يُستخدم مرهم زيلوكايين في تسكين الآلام في مختلف حالات الشرج (على سبيل المثال البواسير، والشقوق، وإفراغ الأمعاء المصحوب بألم).

يمكن صرف مرهم زيلوكايين بدون وصفة طبية في حالة البالغين والأطفال فوق 18 شهراً. الوصفة الطبية ضرورية لصرف الدواء للأطفال أقل من 18 شهراً.

يمكن اعتماد ليدوكايين، وهو أحد مكونات زايلوكايين، لعلاج حالات أخرى غير مدرجة في هذه النشرة. استشر طبيبك، أو الصيدلي، أو طاقم الرعاية الصحية إذا كان لديك أي أسئلة أخرى، والتزم دائماً بنصائحهم.

2.ما الذي تحتاج لمعرفته قبل استخدام زيلوكايين

لا تستخدم زيلوكايين:

-إذا كنت مصاباً بحساسية (حساسية مفرطة) لمادة الليدوكايين أو لأي مادة تخدير موضعي أخرى أو أي من مكونات زيلوكايين الأخرى (المذكورة في القسم 6).

التحذيرات والاحتياطات

-في حالة إصابات الحروق، يجب الامتناع عن استخدام مرهم زيلوكايين على مناطق بحجم أكبر من راحة اليد.

-يجب الامتناع عن استخدام مرهم زيلوكايين للأطفال أقل من 18 شهراً عدا بوصفة من الطبيب.

-تحدث إلى طبيبك قبل استخدام مرهم زيلوكايين إذا كانت كنت تعاني من اضطرابات في جهاز التوصيل الكهربي في القلب لديك (انسداد قلبي)، أو مرض كبدي حاد، أو قصور كلوي حاد، أو اعتلال الصحة العامة.

الأدوية الأخرى وزيلوكايين

أخبر طبيبك أو الصيدلي إن كنت تتناول أو تناولت مؤخراً أو قد تتناول أي أدوية أخرى.

يرتفع احتمال ظهور الآثار الجانبية عند استخدام مرهم زيلوكايين بالتزامن مع أدوية محددة أخرى، بالتحديد هي

  • أدوية التخدير الموضعي الأخرى
  • أدوية علاج عدم انتظام النشاط القلبي (تُسمى مضادات اضطرابات نظم القلب).

الحمل والرضاعة الطبيعية

استشيري طبيبك أو الصيدلي قبل تناول أي دواء.

لا يُحتمل أن يتسبب الاستخدام المؤقت لمرهم زيلوكايين خلال فترة الحمل في إحداث أي آثار ضارة على الجنين.

تُفرز مادة الليدوكايين في الحليب البشري، لكن من غير المحتمل أن تؤثر على الرضيع. رغم ذلك ينبغي استشارة الطبيب في غير حالات الاستخدام المؤقت لزيلوكايين أثناء فترة الرضاعة الطبيعية.

القيادة واستخدام الآلات

عند الاستخدام بالجرعات الموصى بها، لن يؤثر زيلوكايين على قدرتك على قيادة السيارة أو استخدام الآلات.

3. كيفية استخدام زيلوكايين

للجروح المؤلمة أو إصابات الحروق: طبق المرهم مباشرة على الجرح أو موضع الحرق. يمكن أيضاً تطبيق المرهم على ضمادة الجرح التي ستوضع بعد ذلك على موضع الإصابة. يتم الوصول لأقصى درجة تسكين للألم بعد حوالي 5 دقائق. إن ترك المرهم ملمساً لزجاً، يمكن مسحه أو شطفه بالماء.

قبل تنظيف الجروح: اتبع الإجراء ذاته. يمكن البدء في التنظيف بعد حوالي 5 دقائق من تطبيق المرهم على الجرح.

تقرح الحلمات الناتج عن الرضاعة الطبيعية: طبقي المرهم قبل إرضاع الطفل بـ 5 - 10 دقائق وامسحيه جيداً أو اشطفيه بالماء قبل البدء فعلياً في عملية الإرضاع .

لحالات الشرج: طبق المرهم على المناطق المتهجية من مرة إلى ثلاث مرات يومياً. في حالة حركات الأمعاء المصحوبة بألم، يجب تطبيق المرهم قبل حوالي 5 - 10 مرات من استخدام المرحاض.

إن لم تتحسن حالة الشرج بعد فترة من أسبوعين لثلاث، أو في حالة حدوث نزيف شرجي، اتصل بطبيبك.

التزم بالجرعات الأخرى على النحو الذي يصفه الطبيب.

لأسباب صحية، لا تستخدم نفس الأنبوبة لحالات الشرج وعلاج الجروح الأخرى.

في حالة استخدام كمية أكبر من اللازم من زيلوكايين:

إذا استخدمت كمية أكبر من اللازم من الدواء، يجب دائماً الاتصال بالطبيب، أو المستشفى، أو مركز معلومات السموم (هاتف 112) للحصول على استشارة وتقييم درجة الخطر.

إن كان لديك أي استفسارات أخرى حول استخدام هذا الدواء، فاسأل طبيبك أو الصيدلي.

4.الآثار الجانبية المحتملة

مثل جميع الأدوية، يمكن أن يسبب زيلوكايين آثاراً جانبية، على الرغم من أنه ليس بالضرورة أن يعاني منها الجميع.

نادرة (تؤثر على أقل من 1 من كل 1000 شخص): تفاعلات تحسسية، الحالات الأكثر خطورة: صدمة تأقية (تفاعل تحسسي حاد مصحوب، على سبيل المثال، بطفح جلدي، وتورم، وحمى، وصعوبة في التنفس، وإغماء). إذا ظهرت عليك ردود فعل تحسسية حادة مثل هذه، يجب عليك فوراً طلب المساعدة في قسم الطوارئ بالمستشفى.

5.كيفية تخزين زيلوكايين

يجب تخزين المرهم في درجة حرارة الغرفة (بحد أقصى 25 درجة مئوية) للحفاظ على قوامه ناعماً وسلساً.

يُحفظ هذا الدواء بعيداً عن مرأى ومتناول الأطفال.

لا تستخدم هذا الدواء بعد تاريخ انتهاء الصلاحية الموضح على العبوة الخارجية. يشير تاريخ انتهاء الصلاحية إلى آخر يوم من ذلك الشهر.

لا تتخلص من أي أدوية في ماء الصرف الصحي أو مع المخلفات المنزلية. اسأل الصيدلي عن كيفية التخلص من الأدوية التي لم تعد بحاجة إليها. ستساعد هذه الإجراءات في الحفاظ على البيئة.

 Read this leaflet carefully before you start using this product as it contains important information for you

Xylocaine 5% ointment

1 g ointment contains 50 mg lidocaine. For the full list of excipients, see section 6.1.

Ointment

Analgesic ointment for painful wounds, e.g. burns, ulcerous nipples in connection with breastfeeding, and insect bites.

Analgesic ointment for various anal conditions such as haemorrhoids and fissures. Following anorectal procedures or in connection with anorectal exploration.

Xylocaine ointment is intended for adults and children of all ages (for children under the age of 18 months, use only if prescribed by a doctor).


Wound care: Apply the ointment either directly to the irritated area in a thin layer or apply to a wound dressing.

For anal conditions: The ointment is to be applied rectally 1–3 times daily. In the case of painful bowel motions, the ointment should be applied 5–10 minutes prior to using the toilet.

 

Paediatric population

Children ≤ 12 years: No data on plasma concentration are available. From a safety perspective it should be assumed that bioavailability is 100% following application to mucous membranes and compromised skin. A single dose should not exceed 0.1 g ointment/kg body weight (equivalent to 5 mg lidocaine/kg body weight). The minimum dosage interval should be 8 hours.

 

Children > 12 years: Patients weighing less than 25 kg should be given doses in proportion to their weight and physical condition.


Hypersensitivity to amide-type local anaesthetics or to any ingredients in the ointment.

Should not be used on children under the age of 18 months unless prescribed by a doctor.

Caution should be observed in patients with partial or complete heart block if there is a risk of high plasma concentrations due to the method of administration or the dose administered. The elderly and patients with severe hepatic disease, severe renal impairment or poor general condition also require particular attention.

Patients treated with antiarrhythmic class III agents (e.g. amiodarone) should be monitored closely and ECG monitoring should be considered as the cardiac effects may be additive.

Excessive lidocaine doses or inadequate time intervals between doses can lead to high plasma concentrations and severe undesirable effects (see section 4.9, Overdose).

Patients should be urged to closely follow the recommended doses.

Xylocaine ointment should be used with caution in patients with compromised mucosal membranes and/or inflammation at the intended application site.

Xylocainee ointment contains propylene glycol, which can cause skin irritation.


Lidocaine should be used with caution in combination with dental anaesthesia administered by injection, other local anaesthetics or substances structurally related to amide-type local anaesthetics, such as class IB antiarrhythmics, as the toxic effects are additive.

No specific interaction studies with local anaesthetics and class III antiarrhytmics have been carried out, but caution is recommended (see section 4.4, Special warnings and precautions for use).

Medicinal products that inhibit the metabolism of lidocaine (e.g. cimetidine and beta blockers) can cause potentially toxic plasma concentrations when lidocaine is given in repeated high doses over a prolonged period. Such interactions are not clinically relevant in short-term lidocaine therapy at recommended doses.


Pregnancy

There are no adequate data on the treatment of pregnant women with lidocaine.

Lidocaine crosses the placenta. It is reasonable to assume that lidocaine has been used in a large number of pregnant women and women of childbearing age. There is no evidence that lidocaine has a negative impact on reproductive processes, such as increased malformations or direct or indirect effects on the foetus. However, the risks for humans have not been fully investigated.

Animal studies are incomplete with regards to the effects on pregnancy, development of the embryo/foetus, childbirth and postnatal development (see section 5.3).

In the case of temporary use of Xylocaine ointment during pregnancy, the benefit is judged to outweigh potential risks.

Breastfeeding

Lidocaine is excreted in small quantities in human milk. It is unlikely that the infant would be affected when recommended doses are used. It is therefore possible to continue breastfeeding when being treated with Xylocaine ointment.

When Xylocaine ointment is used on ulcerous nipples in connection with breastfeeding, the ointment should be thoroughly wiped off or rinsed off with water prior to breastfeeding.


Xylocaine ointment has no or negligible influence on the ability to drive and use machines.


Adverse reactions to local anaesthetics in the strict sense occur in less than 1 per 1,000 treated patients.

Rare (<1/1,000)

General disorders: Allergic reactions; in the most severe cases: anaphylactic shock.

Adverse reactions of the same type as those arising in cases of absolute overdose of parenterally administered lidocaine may occur in case of overdose of Xylocainee ointment. Systemic toxicity is, however, very unlikely at recommended doses. In case of toxicity, the symptoms are expected to be similar to those seen after other local anaesthetic treatment, i.e. excitatory CNS symptoms, and in rare cases CNS depression and myocardial depression.

Topical administration of 8.6–17.2 mg/kg lidocaine gave very severe toxicity in small children.

Treatment:

If signs of acute systemic toxicity appear, administration of the local anaesthetic must be immediately suspended. Treatment must be given to maintain good ventilation, oxygen supply and circulation. Oxygen is always administered; if required, controlled ventilation is to

be administered. If the convulsions have not ceased spontaneously within 15–20 seconds, administer thiopental sodium 1–3 mg/kg IV to permit adequate ventilation or diazepam

0.1 mg/kg IV (somewhat slower onset of effect). Convulsions of long duration are a risk to the patient’s breathing and oxygenation. The injection of a muscle relaxant (e.g. suxamethonium 1 mg/kg) creates more favourable conditions for ventilation and oxygenation of the patient, but requires experience of tracheal intubation and controlled ventilation. In the case of a drop in blood pressure or bradycardia, administer a vasopressor (e.g. ephedrine 5–10 mg IV, which can be repeated after 2–3 minutes).

In the case of circulatory arrest, cardiopulmonary resuscitation should be commenced immediately. It is important to maintain a good oxygen supply, breathing and circulation, and to treat acidosis.


Pharmacotherapeutic group: Local anaesthetics, ATC code: D04AB01

Xylocaine ointment contains 5% lidocaine dissolved in an ointment base which consists of a mixture of carbowaxes and propylene glycol. Xylocainee ointment has good penetration properties and a rapid anaesthetic effect. Xylocaine ointment is easily washed off with water and does not impede wound healing. The propylene glycol in the ointment base has an antibacterial effect on many pathogenic bacteria.


Paediatric population

Children ≤ 12 years: No data on plasma concentration are available. From a safety perspective it should be assumed that bioavailability is 100% following application to mucous membranes and damaged skin.


Reproduction toxicology

No teratogenic effects were observed in studies of embryo/foetal development in rats and rabbits involving administration during organogenesis. Embryotoxicity was seen in rabbits at a dose causing maternal toxicity. The offspring of rats treated with a dose causing maternal toxicity during late gestation and lactation showed decreased postnatal survival.

Genotoxicity and carcinogenicity

Genotoxicity studies of lidocaine were negative. The carcinogenicity of lidocaine has not been studied. The metabolite of lidocaine 2,6-xylidine has genotoxic potential in vitro. In a carcinogenicity study in rats exposed to 2,6-xylidine in utero, postnatally and over the entire lifespan, tumours in the nostrils, subcutaneous tissue and liver were seen. The clinical relevance of the tumour findings to short-term or intermittent use of lidocaine is unknown.
