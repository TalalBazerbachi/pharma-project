Type 1* poliomyelitis virus (LS –c2ab strain)......................at least 106.0 CCID50** Type 2* poliomyelitis virus (p 712, Ch, 2ab strain)..............at least 105.0 CCID50** Type 3* poliomyelitis virus (Leon 12a1b strain)..................at least 105.8 CCID50** For one dose of 0.1 mL (2 drops) in a multidose container of 10 or 20 doses. * produced on VERO cells **CCID50: infecting dose for 50% of cell cultures (Infectious viral units) For excipients, see section 6.1.

Oral suspension.

This vaccine is indicated for active immunisation against poliomyelitis in newborns, children and adults, either as a primary vaccination or as a booster.
The use of an injectable inactivated poliomyelitis vaccine is preferable for primary vaccination, while the oral poliomyelitis vaccine should be used for boosters or in case of epidemics.


Posology
Primary vaccination consists of 3 doses, which can be administered from 6 weeks of age (or from birth in endemic areas), with a minimum interval of 4 weeks between each dose.
Booster vaccination consists of 1 dose, one year after the third dose, then every 5 years in children and adolescents and every 10 years in adults.
In endemic areas an additional dose may be administered during "national immunisation days" as defined by local authorities.
In case of epidemics: at least one dose must be given to all subjects living in close contact with the case reported, whatever their immunisation status.
Method of administration
The vaccine must be administered orally.
The vaccinating dose is 2 drops (0.1 mL) measured using the drop-counter provided.
The vaccinating dose may be administered directly into the mouth or on a lump of sugar.

In such cases, the inactivated vaccine should be used. This vaccine must not be injected.

In case of vomiting or diarrhoea following administration, a second dose may be administered after the symptoms have disappeared.
The potential risk of apnoea and the need for respiratory monitoring for 48-72 h should be considered when administering the primary immunisation series to very premature infants (born ≤ 28 weeks of gestation) and particularly for those with a previous history of respiratory immaturity. As the benefit of vaccination is high in this group of infants, vaccination should not be withheld or delayed.

Pregnancy
Teratogenic data in animals are lacking.
Clinical epidemiological studies have not revealed any teratogenic or foetotoxic effects related to the use of the poliomyelitis vaccine.
Consequently this vaccine may be prescribed during pregnancy if needed, particularly in case of epidemics.
Lactation
If a lactating woman needs to be vaccinated, the injectable form of poliomyelitis vaccine is preferred.

Pharmacotherapeutic group: POLIOMYELITIS VACCINES,
ATC code: J07BF02.
Vaccine prepared from attenuated strains of types 1, 2 and 3 live poliomyelitis viruses, cultured on VERO cells, filtered and purified.
After administration, the viruses replicate in the intestine and are excreted in faeces. The immune response induced by the vaccine is similar to that induced by the natural infection. It results in the production of circulating antibodies and of secretory immunoglobulins A in the intestine.
Primary vaccination of 3 doses followed by booster doses induces long-term immunity against the 3 viruses in 95% of vaccinated subjects. Local immunity may play a role in the prevention of infections by wild viruses.
Fecal-oral excretion and dissemination may lead to indirect vaccination of contacts. This justifies the particular use of the oral poliomyelitis vaccine during epidemics.
In exceptional cases, a phenomenon of reversion of the virulence may occur for one of the strains, and may cause paralysis in the vaccinated subject or in the subject's close contacts (see 4.8 Undesirable effects).
In cases of insufficient vaccine coverage, epidemics due to viruses derived from a vaccine strain have been described.
