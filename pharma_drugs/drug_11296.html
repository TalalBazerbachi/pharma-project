ANTIDIARRHEAL MICROORGANISMS.
ATC Code: A07FA02 (A: Digestive tract and metabolism).
FLORATIL is used as an adjuvant treatment of diarrhea, in addition to rehydration, in adults and children
above 2 years.
 


If you have been told by your doctor that you have an intolerance to some sugars, contact your doctor
before taking this medicinal product.


DO NOT TAKE FLORATIL 100 mg powder for oral suspension in sachet:
- If you are allergic to one of the ingredients of this medicine
- If you have a central venous catheter, immunodeficiency or are hospitalized (because you are
critically ill or have a weakened immune system).


Be careful with FLORATIL 100 mg, powder for oral suspension in sachet:
 This medicine is contraindicated in patients suffering from lactose, galactose or fructose
intolerance, a Lapp lactase deficiency or glucose-galactose malabsorption (rare hereditary
diseases).


There have been reports in patients
with a central venous catheter, critically ill or immunocompromised patients, even not treated with S.
boulardii, of very rare cases of fungemia (penetration of blood by yeast), most often resulting in
pyrexia and blood cultures positive for Saccharomyces. In most cases, the outcome has been
satisfactory after cessation of treatment with Saccharomyces boulardii, administration of antifungal
treatment and removal of the catheter when necessary. However, the outcome was fatal in some
critically ill patients. As with all medicines made from living microorganisms, special attention must be
paid to the handling of the product in the presence of patients mainly with central venous catheter but
also with peripheral catheter, even not treated with Saccharomyces boulardii, in order to avoid any
contamination by hand and/or the spread of microorganisms by air.

For children between 2 and 6 years, you must immediately consult your physician in the following
cases:
 In case of diarrhea with more than 6 loose stools per day or lasting more than 24 hours or
which is accompanied by a weight loss; your physician decide the necessity to prescribe a
rehydration by oral rehydration solution,
 in case of fever, vomiting;
 in case of presence of blood or mucus in the stools.

For children above 6 years and adults, you must immediately consult your doctor in the following
cases:
 in absence of improvement after 2 days of treatment;
 in case of fever, vomiting;
 in case of presence of blood or mucus in the stools
 in case of intense thirst, dryness of tongue: these signs show the beginning of the dehydration,
that means an important loss of liquid due to the diarrhea. Your physician will decide the
necessity to prescribe a rehydration which could be administered by oral or IV route.

In infants and children below 6 years, it is imperative to respect the terms of use and the mode of
reconstitution of the oral rehydration solution which can be prescribed by your physician and follow his
advice on diet.

In children overs 6 years and adults, this treatment is a complement to dietetic rules:
 rehydration by abundant, salted or sweetened drinks, in order to compensate for the loss of
liquid due to the diarrhoea (the average daily ration of water in adult is 2 litres);
 to feed during the diarrhoea: by excluding certain supply and particularly fruits, green
vegetables, spiced dishes, as well as food or frozen drinks; by privileging roasted meats and
rice. The suppression of milk and dairies should be discussed with your physician.

FLORATIL contains living cells. This medicine should therefore not be mixed with very hot (over
50°C), iced or alcoholic drinks or food.

Taking other medicines
Do not use this medicine at the same time as an antifungal agent (medicine active against fungus
infections).
Please tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking or have recently taken any other medicines,
including medicines obtained without a prescription.

Pregnancy and breastfeeding
It is not recommended to take this medicine during pregnancy.
If you discover that you are pregnant during the treatment, consult your doctor as only he can decide if
it is necessary to go on the treatment.
Ask your physician or your pharmacist for advice before taking any medicine

Special warnings
This medicine contains lactose and fructose. Its use is not advisable in patients not tolerant to galactose or
fructose, having a lactase Lapp deficit or presenting syndromes of bad absorption of glucose, galactose or
fructose (rare heredity diseases).

In children between 2 and 6 years old, rehydration could be the principal element in the treatment of acute
diarrhea in children. It has to be systematically considered.
• Prevention or treatment of dehydration will have to be done by oral rehydration solution. It is
recommended to use solution provided to this purpose and to respect the mode of reconstitution and
administration.
• The Na+ concentration will have to be between 30 and 60 mmol/L, the solutions less concentrated
(30 mmol/L) are designated for not severe dehydration.
• An intake of chlorine and potassium is necessary to correct the digestive loss.
• The recommended concentration in glucose is between 74 and 110 mmol/L
• The intake of hydrolyzed proteins or amino acids does not seem to improve neither rehydration nor
the nutritional state.
• It is essential to propose to the child to drink very often, every quarter of an hour for example. For
guidance, the volume of rehydration solution proposed should be equivalent of the weight loss,
which means 50 to 100mL/kg for a rehydration of 5 to 10 % of body’s weight.
• In case of severe or prolonged diarrhea, or important vomit or the refuse of food, an intravenous
rehydration will have to be considered.

In children above 6 years and adults, if diarrhea persists after 2 days of treatment, management must be
reviewed and the need for rehydration using an oral or intravenous solution envisaged.
There have been very rare cases of fungaemia (and blood cultures positive for Saccharomyces strains) and
sepsis reported mostly in patients with central venous catheter, critically ill or immunocompromised patients,
most often resulting in pyrexia. In most cases, the outcome has been satisfactory after cessation of treatment
by Saccharomyces boulardii, administration of antifungal treatment and removal of the catheter when
necessary. However, the outcome was fatal in some critically ill patients (see sections 4.3 and 4.8).
As with all medicines made from living micro-organisms, special attention must be paid to the handling of the
product in the presence of patients mainly with central venous catheter but also with peripheral catheter,
even not treated with Saccharomyces boulardii, in order to avoid any contamination by hands and/or the
spread of microorganisms by air (see section 4.2).
In addition, enteral administration of Floratil should be avoided because of the risk for environmental
contamination and cross-contamination when the seal of the sachet is opened.

Special precautions for use
Children between 2 and 6 years old in case of prescription of rehydration by solution for rehydration, both
way of use and way of reconstitution will have to be clearly and precisely explained.
However, if there is no necessity to prescribe this kind of rehydration, it is necessary to clearly explain the
need:
- to rehydrate the children by drinking copious amounts of salty or sweet drinks, in order to
compensate for fluid losses due to diarrhea (mean daily water requirement of an adult is 2 liters);
- to eat while they have diarrhea, excluding certain types of food and especially raw salads, fruit,
green vegetables, spicy foods and iced foods or drinks and preferring grilled meats and rice. The
suppression of milk and other dairies should be considered if necessary.
Since FLORATIL consists of living cells that develop at 37°C, do not mix it with a liquid or food which is too
hot (more than 50°C), iced or containing alcohol.
 


Because of its fungal nature, FLORATIL must not be administered with systemic or oral antifungal drugs.
No other drug interactions are expected.
 


Pregnancy
Category C. Animal reproduction studies have not been conducted with FLORATIL.
Clinically, no malformative nor foetotoxic effect has been reported to date. However, the monitoring of
pregnancies exposed to this medicine is insufficient to rule out any risk.
Hence, as a precautionary measure, this medicine should not be used during pregnancy.


Breast-feeding
Saccharomyces boulardii is not absorbed. In the absence of data, it is preferable to weight benefit/risk before
using it during lactation
 


Not relevant.

Pharmaco-therapeutic class: Antidiarrheal microorganisms
Code ATC: A07F A02
A: Digestive tract and metabolism
Flora substitute.
Saccharomyces boulardii is an intestinal replacement flora, which acts as antidiarrhoel microorganisms in the
digestive tract. Pharmacodynamics of Saccharomyces boulardii, live probiotic yeast, have been established
on various models by in vitro and in vivo, animal, and human studies which demonstrates it has
-anti-inflammatory (enterohemorrhagic E. coli)
- anti-microbial (enterohemorrhagic E. coli ; C. difficile; S. typhimurium ; Yersinia enterocolitica ;
C. albicans ; C. krusei ; C. pseudotropicalis)
- enzymatic (disaccharidases; leucine aminopeptidase; spermine; spermidine),
- metabolic,
- anti-toxinic (C. diff. toxin A; cholera toxin),
