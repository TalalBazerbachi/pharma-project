Vibrocil Drops

Active substances Dimethindene maleate, phenylephrine. Excipients Sorbitol, disodium phosphate, citric acid monohydrate, lavender oil, benzalkonium chloride 0.1 mg/mL, purified water.

Nasal drops, solution 1 mL contains: Dimethindene maleate 0.25 mg, phenylephrine 2.5 mg. 1 drop contains about 0.009 mg dimethindene maleate and 0.09 mg phenylephrine.

Vibrocil is indicated for the short-term treatment of the symptoms of a blocked nose and itching
associated, for example, with a cold, sinusitis, allergic rhinitis, hay fever and other upper respiratory tract
illnesses.
Adjuvant in acute otitis media.
For pre-operative and post-operative treatment.
Consult a doctor if there is no improvement after 3 days or if the symptoms get worse.


Do not exceed the stated dose or frequency of use.
The lowest effective dose should always be used for the shortest possible duration of treatment.
Adults and children from 6 years of age (under adult supervision):
3 - 4 times per day, 3 - 4 drops in each nostril.
Children from 2 to 6 years of age (under adult supervision):
3 - 4 times per day, 1 - 2 drops in each nostril.
Children under 2 years of age
Vibrocil nasal drops are not recommended for children under 2 years of age.
Using the drops
• Clean the nose carefully before use (e.g. by blowing it).
• Make sure the drops do not get into the eyes.
• Tip the head back slightly. Insert the drops into each nostril and leave the head tipped
back for a short time to distribute the drops.
• Clean and dry the pipette before screwing it back onto the bottle.
A bottle of Vibrocil nasal drops must only be used by one person to avoid spreading infection.


Hypersensitivity to any of the active substances (phenylephrine, dimethindene maleate) or to any of the excipients in the composition. Contraindicated: • in simple atrophic rhinitis, atrophic rhinitis with foetor (ozena), • in patients who are taking or who have taken monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAO inhibitors) in the last 2 weeks, • in patients with closed angle glaucoma, • after transsphenoidal hypophysectomy (or after transnasal or transoral surgical interventions that expose the dura mater).

Patients who are hypersensitive to sympathomimetics manifested by insomnia, dizziness, tremor,
cardiac arrhythmia or hypertension should only use sympathomimetics like Vibrocil with caution.
Vibrocil should not be used continuously for more than 3 days. Consult a doctor if symptoms persist or worsen. Prolonged or excessive use may lead to tachyphylaxis, rebound congestion or rhinitis
medicamentosa.
As with all topical vasoconstrictors, do not exceed the prescribed dose. This applies in particular to
young children and the elderly, in whom excessive use may lead to systemic effects occurring.
Exercise caution in patients with hypertension, cardiovascular disease, hyperthyroidism, diabetes or
obstruction of the neck of the bladder (e.g. enlarged prostate).
Vibrocil should be used with caution in epileptic patients because of the dimethindene maleate, an H1
antihistamine.
Children
Vibrocil nasal drops are not recommended for children under 2 years of age.
Vibrocil should be used under adult supervision in children between 2 and 12 years of age.
Information about the excipients
Benzalkonium chloride may cause irritation or swelling inside the nose, especially if it is used for a long
time.


Monoamine oxidase inhibitors
Phenylephrine is contraindicated in patients who are taking MAO inhibitors or who have taken them in
the last 2 weeks. Use of MAO inhibitors and sympathomimetic amines such as phenylephrine may
cause a hypertensive reaction (see “Contraindications”).
Tricyclic and tetracyclic antidepressants
Vasoconstrictors should be used with caution in patients who are taking tricyclic and tetracyclic
antidepressants. Concomitant administration may reinforce the hypertensive effect of phenylephrine.
Beta blockers and other antihypertensive medication
Vasoconstrictors should be used with caution in patients who are taking beta blockers or other
antihypertensive medication. Phenylephrine may reduce the effect of beta blockers and other
antihypertensive medication. The risk of hypertension and other cardiovascular side effects may be
increased; use of the product is not recommended.


Pregnancy
No studies are available on the use of phenylephrine and dimethindene maleate in pregnant women.
Therefore, as a precaution, Vibrocil should not be used during pregnancy.
Breast-feeding
No studies are available on the use of phenylephrine and dimethindene maleate while breast-feeding.
Phenylephrine and dimethindene maleate may pass into breast milk. Therefore, as a precaution, Vibrocil
should not be used while breast-feeding.
Effect on the ability to drive and use machinery
No data available.


No data available.


Adverse effects are listed by MedDRA system organ class and by frequency. The information
concerning the type and frequency of adverse effects comes mostly from older studies dating from the
1970s and 1990s.
The frequencies are defined as follows: very common (≥1/10), common (≥1/100 to <1/10), uncommon
(≥1/1000 to <1/100), rare (≥1/10,000 to <1/1000), very rare (<1/10,000). Within each frequency
grouping, adverse effects are shown in order of decreasing severity.
Respiratory, thoracic and mediastinal disorders
Rare: nasal problems, nasal dryness, epistaxis.
General disorders and problems at the administration site
Rare: burning sensation at the administration site.
The reporting of suspected adverse effects that occur after authorisation is extremely important. It
enables the benefit-risk balance of the medicinal product to be evaluated continuously. Healthcare
professionals are required to report all suspected new or serious adverse effects via the online reporting
system ElViS (Electronic Vigilance System). Information on this subject can be found at www.swissmedic.ch.


Signs and symptoms
An overdose of Vibrocil may trigger sympathomimetic and anticholinergic effects such as palpitations,
ventricular extrasystoles, mild sinus tachycardia, hypertension, occipital headache, tremor, mydriasis,
dizziness, pallor, agitation, hallucinations, cramps, insomnia, but also sedation, fatigue, coma,
abdominal pain, nausea and vomiting.
To date, no severe side effects have been observed following the accidental ingestion of Vibrocil.
Accidental ingestion of up to 20 mg of dimethindene has not resulted in severe symptoms.
Treatment
No special measures are required in children after the ingestion of a whole bottle (15 mL) or tube (12 g)
of Vibrocil.
Given the lack of data for doses greater than 20 mg of dimethindene, medical supervision and, after
discussion with a clinical expert or the poison control centre, administration of a single dose of activated
charcoal are indicated.
Severe cases of agitation and cramps must be treated with a benzodiazepine. Use of the following
antidotes must be discussed with a clinical expert or the poison control centre: central anticholinergic
symptoms may be treated with physostigmine. Hypertension triggered by peripheral sympathomimetic
stimulation which does not respond sufficiently to benzodiazepines can be treated with an alpha blocker.


Mechanism of action
Vibrocil unblocks the nose and stops nasal discharge without disrupting the physiology of the cilia in the
nasal mucosa. Phenylephrine is a sympathomimetic amine. Due to its selective action on the alphaadrenergic
receptors of the venous erectile tissues of the nasal mucosa, phenylephrine is a gentle
vasoconstrictor that allows rapid and lasting decongestion of the nostrils.
Dimethindene maleate, a histamine H1 receptor antagonist, is an effective anti-allergic substance at low
doses that is well tolerated.

Clinical efficacy

This medicinal product is intended for topical application. Its efficacy is not correlated with the blood
concentration of the active substances.
In the event of accidental ingestion, the bioavailability of phenylephrine is reduced (approximately 38%)
due to the first-pass effect in the intestine and liver. The elimination half-life is approximately 2.5 hours.
The systemic availability of dimethindene in oral solutions is approximately 70%. The elimination half-life
is approximately 6 hours.
