Aciloc represent a new trend In the treatment of peptic and allied conditions whereby the final step of gastric acid
secretion is inhibited irresepective of the stimulus. Aciloc m acts specifically by inhibiting the H+/K• ATP-ase enzyme
system at the secretory surface of the gastric parietal cells, blocking thereby the final transport of hydrogen ions into the
gastric lumen, and thus inhibiting effectively both basal and simulated acid secretion. The onset of action is rapid and
reversible control of gastric acid secretion achieved with once daily dosing. Aciloc has no effect on acetylcholine or
histamine receptors.


Hypersensitivity to omeprazole. Omeprazole Is better to be avoided during pregnancy and lactation, until further studies
are available.

As general practice In treating gastric ulcer. the possibility of gastric malignancy should be excluded prior to the initiation
of therapy with omeprazole.


Duodenal ulcer, gastric ulcer and reflux esophagitis: The recommended dose is one Aciloc w 20 mg capsule once daily
preferably in the morning. In severe cases or cases refractory to other treatment regimens, the dosage can be
increased to Acilocw 40 mg capsule in the morning- healing usually starts to occur within 2 weeks In patients with
duodenal ulcer, or 4 weeks in patients having gastric ulcer or reflux esophagitis. For those patients who may not
have fully healed after the initial course, healing Usually occurs during a further course of treatment (4-8 weeks from
the start of therapy). For maintenance therapy to avoid recurrence of episode especially in acid reflux disease, a daily
dose of 10-20 mg is recommended (Therapy could be continued for as long as it is clinically indicated).
Gastric hypersecretory conditions: up to 120 mg daily, in divided doses; the dosage being adjusted as needed and
therapy continued for as long as clinically indicated. Antacids may be given concomitantly, especially with the first few
doses, to aid in the relief of pain. The capsules should be swallowed whole with some fluids before meals,


Omeprazole Is usually well tolerated. Some minor adverse events have been occasionally reported, such as abdominal
pain, tiredness, headache, dizziness, constipation, diarrhea, flatulence, nausea, vomiting, and skin rashes.
Those adverse reactions are transient and reversible in nature.


Store below 30°C, Keep out of the reach of children.
