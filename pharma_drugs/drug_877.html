  SFDA PIL (Patient Information Leaflet (PIL) are under review by Saudi Food and Drug Authority)

This product is a TOPICAL ANTIFUNGAL which is used for treatment of nail mycoses (fungal disorders).


a. Do not use Loceryl if you are

- If you are allergic to amorolfine or one of the other ingredients contained in this medicine, as listed in section 6.
- If in doubt, ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice.

b. Take special care with Loceryl.

- By avoiding contact with the eyes, ears and mucous membranes.

- If you are diabetic.

- If you are being treated for an immune system disease (a disease that reduces your body's natural defenses).

- If you have poor circulation in your hands or feet.

- If your nail is severely damaged (more than two thirds of your nail is affected) or infected. In these cases, your doctor may prescribe you an oral treatment in addition to the mdeical lacquer, waterproof gloves must be worn or the lacquer will be dissolved.

c. Taking other medicines with Loceryl.

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are currently taking, have recently taken, or might take any other medicinal product.

d. Pregnancy and Breastfeeding.

There is limited data available on the use of amorolfine in pregnant and breastfeeding women.

Loceryl must not be used during pregnancy and breastfeeding, unless it is really necessary.

If you are pregnant or breastfeeding, if you think you may be pregnant or you plan to become pregnant, talk to your doctor or pharmacist prior to using any medicines.

e. Important information about some of the ingredients of Loceryl

Loceryl contains ethanol (alcohol). Prolonged or improper use may result in irritation or dryness of the skin surrounding the nail.


Apply Loceryl on affected nails once or twice per week, with normal usage; this bottle contains enough for approximately 134 applications.

Follow the direction of your doctor or pharmacist.

Method and route of aministration

To apply the lacquer, carefully follow the instructions of use.

Instructions for proper use

Fpr packages without accessories:

1. Before applying Loceryl for the first time, clean the nails well and file the infected area of the nail (especailly the surface of the nail), as thoroughly as possible. Take care not to file the periungual skin.

2. Clean the surface of the nail and remove oil using a compress or tissue dampened with nail polish remover.

3. Paint the lacquer over the entire surface of the affected nail using one of the reusable spatulas provided. Always clean the reusable spatula before moving on to the next nail, in order to avoid contaminating the lacquer. Do not wipe the spatula on the edge of the bottle.

4. Clean the spatula with a pad or tissue soaked with nail polish remover.

5. Close the vial tightly.

Repeat all the above steps for each infected nail.

Special instructions:

  • Do not reuse the files on healthy nails
  • Before each new application, file with nail varnish remover to remove any residual lacquer.
  • Waterproof gloves should be worn when handling organic solvents (white spirit, thinners...) to protect the coat of LOCERYL lacquer on the nails.
  • Cosmetic nail lacquers may be applied at least 10 min after LOCERYL nail lacquer application.

 For packages with accessories:

a) Before first use of LOCERYL, carefully clean and prepare the nails, filing down the affected areas (especially the surface of the nail) as thoroughly as possible.

Take care not to file the periungual skin.

b) Clean the surface of the nail and remove oil using one of the cleansing wipes provided, in order to remove any residual lacquer.

c) Paint the lacquer over the entire surface of the infected nail using one of the reusable spatulas provided. Always clean the reusable spatula before moving on to the next nail, in order to avoid contaminating the lacquer. Do not wipe the spatula on the edge of the bottle.

d) Clean the spatula with one of the cleansing wipes provided. Tightly close the bottle.

Repeat all the above steps for each infected nail.

Frequency of administration

Treatment should be continued without interruption until the nail has fully regrown and recovered.

Duration of treatment

Treatment generally lasts for 6 months for fingernails and 9 months for toenails.

a. If you use more Loceryl® than you should

Immediately contact your doctor or pharmacist.

b. If you forget to use Loceryl®

Don’t worry if you forget to use Loceryl at the right time. When you remember, start using the product again, in the same way as before.

c. If you stop using Loceryl®

Do not stop using Loceryl before your doctor tells you to or your infection could come back.

If you have any further questions on the use of this medicine, ask your doctor or pharmacist.


Like all medicines, LOCERYL 5% medicinal nail lacquer can cause adverse effects, although not everybody gets them.

- Rare adverse effects (affect less than 1 in 1,000 people):

  • Nail damage, discolouration of the nails, brittle or weak nails

 - Very rare adverse effects (affect less than 1 in 10,000 people):

  • Skin burning sensation.

- Adverse effects of unknown frequency (cannot be determined based on the available data):

  • Redness, itching, urticaria (hives), skin vesicles, contact allergies
  • Systemic allergic reaction (a serious allergic reaction that can be associated with swelling of the face, lips, tongue or throat, difficulty breathing and/or a severe skin rash)

- All medicines can cause allergic reactions; although most are mild, some can be serious. If this happens, stop applying LOCERYL, immediately remove LOCERYL with a nail varnish remover or the cleaning swabs provided with the package and seek medical advice. LOCERYL must not be reapplied.

- You must get immediate medical help if you have any of the following symptoms:

   - You have difficulty breathing

   - Your face, lips, tongue or throat swell

   - Your skin develops a severe rash.

Reporting of side effects:

If you get any side effects, talk to your doctor or pharmacist. This includes any possible side effects not listed in this leaflet.

By reporting side effects you can help provide more information on the safety of this medicine.

Medicated nail lacquer

First line treatment of onychomycoses without involvement of the nail matrix (onychostroma).


Posology:
Apply LOCERYL on affected nails once or twice per week.
Method of administration:
To apply the lacquer, follow these recommendations carefully:

For packaging without accessories:
a) Before the first application of LOCERYL, carefully clean the nails and file the affected area of the nail (especially the nail surface) as completely as possible. Take care not to file the skin around the nail.
b) Clean the surface of the nail and remove any grease with a compress or tissue impregnated with nail varnish remover.
c) Apply the lacquer using one of the reusable spatulas over the whole of the affected nail. Clean the reusable spatula every time you move from one nail to another to avoid contaminating the lacquer. Do not wipe the spatula on the edge of the bottle.
d) It is important to clean the reusable spatula with a compress or tissue impregnated with nail varnish remover after
each application, before reusing it on another nail, to avoid contaminating the lacquer.
e) If lacquer has been deposited on the outside of the stopper during handling, it is important to clean it off using a compress or tissue impregnated with nail varnish remover, in order to avoid contact with the skin.
f) Recap the bottle tightly.
Repeat all of the above steps for each affected nail.

Cosmetic nail varnish may be applied at least 10 minutes after application of the LOCERYL lacquer. The treatment must be continued without interruption until complete regeneration of the nail and clinical and mycological healing of the affected areas.
The duration of treatment is generally 6 months for fingernails and 9 months for toenails (this depends mainly on the severity, the site of the infection and the height of the nail involvement).
Specific instructions:

• Do not re-use nail files for healthy nails.
• Before each new application, file the affected nails if necessary, and in all cases clean the nails with nail varnish remover to eliminate any residual lacquer.
• When handling organic solvents (white spirit, thinners, etc.), wear impermeable gloves to protect the LOCERYL lacquer layer on the nails.

For packaging with accessories:
a) Before the first application of LOCERYL, carefully clean the nails and file the affected area of the nail (especially the nail surface) as completely as possible.
Take care not to file the skin around the nail.
b) Clean the surface of the nail and remove any grease using one of the cleansing wipes provided to remove any residual varnish.
c) Apply the lacquer using one of the reusable spatulas on the entire affected nail. Clean the reusable spatula every
time you move from one nail to another to avoid contaminating the lacquer. Do not wipe the spatula on the edge of the bottle.
d) It is important to clean the reusable spatula thoroughly with one of the cleansing wipes provided after each application, before reusing it on another nail, in order to avoid contaminating the lacquer.
e) If lacquer has been deposited on the outside of the stopper during handling, it is important to clean it off using one of the cleansing wipes provided in order to avoid contact with the skin.
f) Recap the bottle tightly.
Repeat all of the above steps for each affected nail.
Cosmetic nail varnish may be applied at least 10 minutes after application of LOCERYL lacquer.
The treatment must be continued without interruption until complete regeneration of the nail and clinical and mycological healing of the affected areas.
The duration of treatment is generally 6 months for fingernails and 9 months for toenails (this depends mainly on the severity, the site of the infection and the height of the nail involvement).

Hypersensitivity to the active substance or to any of the excipients listed in section 6.1.

Special warnings:
Avoid any contact of LOCERYL with the eyes, ears or mucous membranes.

LOCERYL must be used with care in patients suffering from diseases which predispose them to nail disorders (peripheral circulation disorders, diabetes, immune system disorders) as well as in patients with nail dystrophy or with seriously damaged nails (more than two thirds of the nail plate affected). In these cases a systemic treatment should be considered.
Do not use false nails during use of LOCERYL.
When handling organic solvents, wear impermeable gloves or the lacquer will be dissolved. After applying LOCERYL lacquer, wait at least 10 minutes before applying a cosmetic nail varnish. Before repeating the LOCERYL treatment, the cosmetic nail varnish must be carefully removed.
A systemic or local allergic reaction may occur after use of this medicinal product. If this happens, stop using it immediately and consult a doctor. Carefully remove the product using nail varnish remover. The medicinal product must not be used again.

Precautions for use:
In the absence of data, use of LOCERYL is not recommended in patients aged under 18 years.


The data available to date do not suggest the existence of clinically significant interactions.


There are limited data available on the use of amorolfine in pregnant and breast-feeding women. Only a small number of pregnancies exposed to topical amorolfine have been reported after market launch, so the potential risk is unknown.
Animal studies have shown reproductive toxicity at high oral doses (see section 5.3).
It is not known whether amorolfine is excreted in breast milk.
LOCERYL should not be used during pregnancy and breast-feeding, except when absolutely necessary.

Pharmacotherapeutic group: OTHER TOPICAL ANTIFUNGAL AGENTS, ATC code: 001AE16.
LOCERYL is a topical antimycotic. The active ingredient, amorolfine, derived from morpholine, belongs to a new chemical class of antimycotics.
Its antifungal and fungicidal effect is based on an alteration of the fungal cell membrane directed mainly at sterol biosynthesis.
The ergosterol content is reduced. An accumulation of atypical sterols leads to morphological changes to the cell membranes and cellular organelles, causing the death of the fungal cell.
Amorolfine has a broad antimycotic spectrum. It is highly effective against the usual or occasional agents of onychomycoses:

• Yeasts:
o Candida albicans and other Candida species,
• Dermatophytes:
o Trichophyton rubrum, Trichophyton interdigitale and Trichophyton mentagrophytes, other Trichophyton
species,
o Epidermophyton floccosum,
o Microsporum.
• Moulds:
o Scopulariopsis.
• Dematiaceous fungi (black fungi):
o Hendersonula, Alternaria, Cladosporium

• Species with low sensitivity:
Aspergillus, Fusarium, Mucorales.


Amorolfine in nail lacquer form penetrates and diffuses through the nail plate and is capable of eradicating the less accessible fungi of the nail bed.
The systemic absorption of the active ingredient is negligible, the plasma concentration remaining below the detection limit, even after one year of use.


In rats, amorolfine is not teratogenic and not embryotoxic at the maximum oral dose tested of 40 and 80mg/kg/day.
However, signs of maternal toxicity (weight loss) have been observed with doses of 40 and 80 mg/kg/day.
In rabbits, amorolfine is not teratogenic, but has an embryotoxic effect at the maximum oral dose tested of 10 mg/kg/day. Weight loss in mothers, indicating maternal toxicity, has also been observed at a dose of 10 mg/kg/day.
Due to the very low systemic absorption of amorolfine following application to a nail or the skin, the potential toxicity risk for the human foetus is negligible.
