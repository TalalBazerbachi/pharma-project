Xylomet nasal drops is a medicine which is used to help clear the stuffy, blocked up feeling in your nose and sinuses caused by colds and allergies such as hay fever.

The drops contains Xylometazoline hydrochloride, which is a topical decongestant that relieves nasal and sinus congestion.


 

This medicine is suitable for most people but a few people should not use it. If you are in any doubt, talk to your doctor or pharmacist.

Do not take this medicine if.

  • You are allergic to Xylometazoline hydrochloride or any of the ingredients. (See Section 6)
  • You are taking, or have taken in the last two weeks, drugs for depression known as Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors (MAOIs).
  • You have recently had neurosurgery.
  • You have had your pituitary gland removed.

If any of these apply to you, get advice from a doctor or pharmacist without taking Xylomet nasal drops.

Talk to your doctor or pharmacist.

  • If you have high blood pressure or heart disease.
  • If you have diabetes.
  • If you have an overactive thyroid gland.
  • If you are pregnant or breast feeding.
  • If you are taking beta blockers.
  • If you have glaucoma.

If any of these bullet points apply to you now or in the past, talk to a doctor or pharmacist.

If you are pregnant or breast-feeding

If you are pregnant or breast-feeding, consult your doctor before using Xylomet.


 

Follow the instructions below to ensure that you use Xylomet drops correctly:

  • Do not use more than the stated dose shown in the table.
 Age Dose

Adults and children aged 12 years and over

  •  2 to 3 drops (adult from 0.1%) into each nostril 2 or 3 times
  • Do not take more than 3 doses in 24 hours.
  • Do not take Xylomet Adult (0.1%) for more than 7 days in arow.

If symptoms persist talk to your doctor.

Caution is recommended in patients with:

–  Hypertension.

–  Cardiovascular diseases.

–  Hyperthyroidism.

–  Diabetes mellitus.

If you take too much

If anyone has too much contact your doctor or nearest Accident & Emergency Department (Casualty) taking this leaflet and pack with you.

If you forget to take the medicine

If you forget to take a dose, take the next dose when needed provided that you do not take more than2- 3 doses in 24 hours. Do not take a double dose.


 

Xylomet nasal drops can have side-effects, like all medicines, although these don't affect everyone and are usually mild.

  • Occasionally people may experience local irritation such as burning, soreness, dryness, stinging, itching, sneezing, nausea, headache or dryness of the nasal mucosa.
  • Nausea and headache may rarely occur.
  • If this medicine is used continuously for long periods, congestion symptoms may return.
  • Excessive use may cause rebound congestion or chemical rhinitis.
  • Systemic cardiovascular effects have occurred in isolated cases.
  • Systemic allergic reactions and transient visual disturbances.

 

- Store below 30°C.

- Keep the product out of the sight and reach of children.

- Do not use your medicine after expiry date on the packaging or the plastic container.

- In use instructions: use during one month after first opening.

- Medicines should not be disposed of via wastewater or household waste. Ask your pharmacist how to dispose of medicines no longer required. These measures will help to protect the environment.

XYLOMET ADULT NASAL DROPS

Xylometazoline Hydrochloride 0.1% w/v For excipients see 6.1.

Nasal drops, solution The product is a clear, colourless solution free from particles.

For the symptomatic relief of nasal congestion, perennial and allergic rhinitis (including hay fever), sinusitis.


Adults and elderly (all indications): 2 or 3 drops in each nostril 2 or 3 times daily.
The drops are suitable for children over 12 years of age.
Route of administration: Nasal use.


Known hypersensitivity to Xylomet. Patients with trans-sphenoidal hypophysectomy or surgery exposing the dura mater.

Patients are advised not to take decongestants for more than seven consecutive days. Xylomet, like other preparations belonging to the same class of active substances, should be used only with caution in patients showing a strong reaction to sympathomimetic agents as evidenced by signs of insomnia, dizziness etc. Caution is recommended in patients with hypertension, cardiovascular disease, hyperthyroidism, narrow angle glaucoma or diabetes mellitus.

Label warnings and precautions

  • Do not exceed the stated dose
  • Do not use continuously for more than seven consecutive days. If symptoms persist, consult your doctor
  • If you are pregnant or taking other medicines or are under a doctor's care, consult your doctor before using Xylomet
  • Not to be used for infants or children under 12 years
  • Each Xylomet pack should be used by one person only to prevent any cross infection
  • For reasons of hygiene do not use this bottle for more than 28 days after first opening it
  • Keep medicines out of the reach of children

Additional leaflet warnings and precautions

  • Do not use if you are sensitive to any of the ingredients of Xylomet
  • Do not use if you have had recent neurosurgery
  • Consult your doctor before using Xylomet if you have heart or circulatory disease
  • Some patients who have sensitive nasal passages may feel some local discomfort when applying the product.
  • Other side effects such as palpitations, nausea and headache are very rare

As for all sympathomimetics, a reinforcement of the systemic effects of xylometazoline by concomitant use of monoamine oxidase inhibitors, tricyclic or tetracyclic antidepressants, cannot be excluded, especially in case of overdose.


No foetal toxicity or fertility studies have been carried out in animals. In view of its potential systemic vasoconstrictor effect, it is advisable to take the precaution of not using Xylomet during pregnancy.
Label warning: If you are pregnant or taking any other medicines, or are under a doctor's care, consult him before using Xylomet.
No evidence of any adverse effect on the breast-fed infant. However, it is not known if xylometazoline is excreted in breast milk, therefore caution should be exercised and Xylomet should be used only on the advice of a doctor whilst breastfeeding.


None known


The following side effects have occasionally been encountered: A burning sensation in the nose and throat, local irritation, nausea, headache and dryness of the nasal mucosa.
Systemic cardiovascular effects have occurred, and this should be kept in mind when giving Xylomet to people with cardiovascular disease. In isolated cases, systemic allergic reactions and transient visual disturbances.

In rare instances of accidental poisoning in children, the clinical picture has been marked chiefly by signs such as acceleration and irregularity of the pulse, elevated blood pressure and sometimes consciousness clouding. There is no specific treatment. Appropriate supportive measures should be initiated.


Xylomet Adult Nasal Drops is a sympathomimetic agent with marked alpha- adrenergic activity, and is intended for use in the nose. It constricts the nasal blood vessels, thereby decongesting the mucosa of the nose and neighbouring regions of the pharynx. This enables patients suffering from colds to breathe more easily through the nose. The effect of Xylomet Adult Nasal Drops begins within a few minutes and lasts for up to 10 hours. Xylomet Adult Nasal Drops is generally well tolerated and does not impair the function of ciliated epithelium. In a double-blind, saline solution (Otrisal) controlled study in patients with common cold, the decongestant effect of Xylomet was significantly superior (p<0.0001) to Otrisal saline solution based on rhinomanometry measurement at 1 hour after administration of the study drugs.


Systemic absorption may occur following nasal application of xylometazoline hydrochloride solutions. It is not used systemically.


There are no findings in the preclinical testing which are of relevance to the prescriber.
