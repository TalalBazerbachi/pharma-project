1 ml (corresponding to 45 drops) contains 4,500 IU Vitamin D 3 (Colecalciferol) in alcoholic solution, 1 drop = 100 IU. Each dose (4 drops) contains 0.05 g alcohol (94% ethanol).

 

VI-DE 3 is for the following :

-        Prevention of rickets

-        Treatment of all forms and stages of rickets

-        Osteomalacia


Do not use VI-DE 3 :

 

Patients with renal or other concomitant diseases

In patients with chronic renal insufficiency, as well as with existing calcium metabolism disorders or coronary diseases, caution with higher doses of vitamin-D is advised.

 

Warnings and precautions

 

Overdosing of vitamin-D in any form is dangerous. Therefore, advise the parents that the following should be avoided:

-        The uncontrolled, prophylactic use of high doses of vitamin-D without specific indication.

-        Other vitamin-D supplements such as vitamin-D containing multivitamin preparations, cod liver oil, excessive vitamin-D enriched diet or calcium-rich diet and excessive radiation.

 

Vi-De 3 drops should only be prescribed with caution in patients who suffer from sarcoidosis as there is a risk of increased metabolism of vitamin-D to its active metabolite. In these patients, the calcium level should be monitored in urine and plasma.

 

Caution is required in patients with disturbances of calcium metabolism, kidney failure, kidney stones, arteriosclerosis or coronary disease. All forms of vitamin-D are toxic in high doses, causing a significant increase in calcium absorption.

 

 

In patients with renal impairment, vitamin-D and calcium salts should be taken under doctor’s supervision of the serum levels of calcium and phosphate.

 

In patients with severe renal insufficiency (patients on dialysis) vitamin-D is not regularly metabolized in the form of cholecalciferol, so other forms of vitamin-D should be given.

 

In case of highly dosed vitamin-D doses, monitoring of the patient is necessary and attention must be paid to the Sulkowich reaction in urine.

 

Toxic effects also occur if doses of 1000-3000 IU/kg bodyweight daily are administered over a period of several months. The fact that milk, fats and baby or other food are often enriched with vitamin-D should be taken into account when determining dosage.

Caution should be exercised in patients receiving treatment with cardiac glycosides since hypercalcaemia may lead to arrhythmia in such cases.

 

Particular care should be taken to avoid the following:

-        Prophylactic administration of vitamin-D in large doses over a period of months or even years without proper monitoring or a specific indication, especially in conjunction with calcium and/or vitamin enriched foods (e.g. baby foods).

-        Massive doses of vitamin-D at short intervals.

This is because the body’s attempts to eliminate the resultant calcium excess might overload the excretory capacity of the kidneys; the first symptoms of over-dosage are polyuria and polydipsia.

 

Patients receiving the recommended dose of Vi-De 3 for prevention of rickets or osteomalacia should avoid other drugs containing vitamin-D and vitamin-D enriched foods.

 

Particular care should be exercised in the prophylactic use of vitamin-D preparations in infants receiving vitamin-D enriched baby foods (normally containing 400 IU per daily portion).

 

Regular blood calcium checks should be performed on patients receiving long-term treatment for vitamin-D resistant rickets.

 

 

Other medicines and VI-DE 3

Phenytoin, Phenobarbital barbiturates, glutethimide and rifampicin (and other liver-enzymes inducing agents) accelerate the degradation of cholecalciferol.

 

Cholestyramine, colestipol and mineral oils reduce the intestinal absorption of vitamin-D and as long an interval as possible (at least 4 hours) should therefore be allowed between ingestion.

 

There is an increased risk of hypercalcemia when vitamin-D is administered concurrently with thiazide diuretics, calcium or phosphate. Possibly necessitating the withdrawal of Vi-De-3.

 

The toxicity of cardiac glycosides may increase as a result of an increase in calcium levels during treatment with vitamin-D (risk of cardiac arrhythmia). Patients should be monitored by ECG and calcium levels in plasma and urine.

 

Functional antagonism with respect to inhibition and promotion of calcium desorption exists between corticosteroids and cholecalciferol.

 

Pregnancy and breast-feeding

Pregnancy:

There are no adequate data for the use of Vi-De 3 drops in pregnant women. However, animal experiments have shown that reproductive toxicity is associated with high doses of cholecalciferol.

There is clear evidence that doses in excess of the daily requirements represent a risk to the foetus.

 

 

Breast-feeding:

Since vitamin-D is excreted in the mother's milk, caution should be taken during breastfeeding.

 

Driving and using machines

No available data


Always use this medicine exactly as your doctor or pharmacist has told you. Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you are not sure.

 

Prevention of rickets

-   Full term infants:

400 IU (= 4 drops) daily in the first year of life, starting in week 2-5

-   Preterm infants:

400-800 IU (= 4-8 drops) daily in the first year of life

Treatment of rickets:

-   Dosage should be individualized on the basis of frequent determinations of serum calcium and bone X-rays

 

Follow-up measurements should be made approximately three to four months after initiating    maintenance therapy to confirm that the target level has been achieved


Like all medicines, this medicine can cause side effects although not everybody gets them.

 

Metabolism and nutrition disorders:

Undesirable effects are not to be expected when the dosage regulations are observed. The side effects arise as a result of overdosing. Symptoms of hypervitaminosis may occur incase of long-term overdose.

They manifest themselves in uncharacteristic complaints such as headache, loss of appetite, weakness, weight loss, stomach and intestinal disorders (emesis, constipation) and growth disorders.

In case of prolonged hypercalcemia, polyuria, polydipsia, nocturia, albuminuria, anorexia, hypercholesterolemia, elevated transaminase levels, cardiac arrhythmia, Hypertension and X-ray detectable soft tissue calcifications may occur.


  • Keep this medicine out of the sight and reach of children.
  • Do not use this medicine after the expiry date which is stated on the bottle and the carton after EXP. The expiry date refers to the last day of that month.
  • Do not store above 30°C.
  • Throw away the bottle 4 weeks after first opening to prevent infections, and use a new bottle. Write down the date of opening on the bottle label and carton label in the space provided.
  • Do not throw away any medicines via wastewater or household waste. Ask your pharmacist how to throw away medicines you no longer use. These measures will help protect the environment.

تحذيرات واحتياطات

 

تُعَد الجرعات الزَّائدة من فيتامين د خطيرة بأي شكل من الأشكال. لذا يُنصَح الوالدان بتجنُّب ما يلي:

-        الاستخدام غير المنضبط والوقائي لجرعات مرتفعة من فيتامين د بدون وجود داعي استعمال مُحَدد.

-        مُكمِّلات فيتامين د الأخرى مثل المُستَحضرات متعددة الفيتامينات التي تحتوي على فيتامين د، زيت كبد الحوت، اتباع نظام غذائي يحتوي على كمية زائدة من فيتامين د أو نظام غذائي غني بالكالسيوم والتعرُّض الزائد للإشعاع.

 

يجب ألا يُوصَف عقار في – دي ٣ قطرات إِلَّا بحذر في المرضى ممن يُعانون من الساركويد؛ لأنَّ هناك خطورة تتمثل في زيادة استقلاب فيتامين د إلى مُستَقلبه النشط. في هؤلاء المرضى، يجب مراقبة مستوى الكالسيوم في البول والبلازما.

 

يجب توخي الحذر في المرضى المُصابين باضطرابات استقلاب الكالسيوم أو فشل الكُلى أو حصى الكُلى أو تصلُّب الشرايين أو مرض بالشريان التَّاجي. يكون فيتامين د سامًّا بجميع أشكاله عند تناوُله بجرعات مرتفعة، وهو ما يُسبب زيادة كبيرة في امتصاص الكالسيوم.

 

في المرضى المُصابين بقصور كُلوي، يجب تناوُل فيتامين د وأملاح الكالسيوم تحت إشراف الطبيب على مستويات الكالسيوم والفوسفات في المصل.

 

في المرضى المُصابين بقصور شديد في وظائف الكُلى (المرضى الذين يخضعون لغسيل الكُلى) لا يُستَقلَب فيتامين د بصفة منتظمة في شكل كوليكالسيفيرول؛ لذا يجب إعطاء أشكال أخرى من فيتامين د.

 

في حال تناوُل جرعات مرتفعة من فيتامين د، تكون مراقبة المريض ضرورية ويجب الانتباه إلى تفاعل سولكوفيتش في البول.

 

تحدث أيضًا آثار سامة إذا تم إعطاء جرعات قدرها 1000-3000 وحدة دولية/ كجم من وزن الجسم يوميًّا على مدار عدة أشهر. يجب أن يُؤخذ في الاعتبار عند تحديد الجرعة حقيقة أنَّ الحليب والدهون وطعام الأطفال أو غيره من المواد الغذائية هي مواد تكون غنية في كثير من الأحيان بفيتامين د.

يجب توخي الحذر في المرضى الذين يتلقون علاجًا بالجليكوزيدات القلبية؛ لأنَّ فرط الكالسيوم بالدَّم قد يُؤدي إلى التعرُّض لاضطرابات النظم القلبي في مثل هذه الحالات.

 

يجب توخي الحذر بشكل خاص لتجنُّب ما يلي:

-        الاستخدام الوقائي لفيتامين د بجرعات مرتفعة على مدار أشهر أو حتى سنوات بدون الخضوع لمراقبة ملائمة أو دون وجود داعي استعمال مُحَدد، لا سيَّما بالاقتران مع الكالسيوم و/أو الأطعمة الغنية بالفيتامينات (على سبيل المثال: أطعمة الأطفال).

-        تناوُل جرعات كبيرة من فيتامين د على فواصل زمنية قصيرة.

 

ويرجع ذلك إلى أنَّ الجسم يحاول إزالة ناتج الكالسيوم الزَّائد مما قد يُؤدي إلى فرط التَّحميل على القدرة الإفرازية للكُلى؛ الأعراض الأولى للجرعة الزَّائدة هي التَّبول المتكرر والعطاش.

 

يجب على المرضى الذين يتناولون الجرعة المُوصى بها من عقار في – دي ٣ للوقاية من الكُسَاح أو تَلَيُّن العِظَامِ تجنُّب تناوُل عقاقير أخرى تحتوي على فيتامين د أو تناوُل أطعمة غنية بفيتامين د.

 

يجب توخي حذر خاصٍّ عند الاستخدام الوقائي لمستحضرات فيتامين د في الرُّضَّع الذين يتلقون أطعمة أطفال غنية بفيتامين د (تحتوي عادةً الحصة اليومية على 400 وحدة دولية).

 

يجب إجراء فحوصات منتظمة؛ لمعرفة مستوى الكالسيوم بالدَّم في المرضى الذين يتلقون علاجًا طويل الأمد لما يُعانونه من كُسَاح مقاوم لفيتامين د.

Patients with renal or other concomitant diseases

In patients with chronic renal insufficiency, as well as with existing calcium metabolism disorders or coronary diseases, caution with higher doses of vitamin-D is advised.


 Known hypersensitivity to a component of the preparation.  Idiopathic hypercalcemia of the infant or toddler.  Diseases with pre-existing hypercalcemia or hypercalcuria.  Severe renal impairment.  Kidney stones or the tendency to form calcium-containing kidney stones.  Pseudohypoparathyroidism.  Severe arteriosclerosis.  Active or non-consolidated lung tuberculosis, which may be reactivated by high vitamin D doses.  Sarcoidosis (Boeck’s disease)  Immobilization following corrective orthopedic surgery  Concomitant administration of vitamin-D analogues

Overdosing of vitamin-D in any form is dangerous. Therefore, advise the parents that the following should be avoided:

-        The uncontrolled, prophylactic use of high doses of vitamin-D without specific indication.

-        Other vitamin-D supplements such as vitamin-D containing multivitamin preparations, cod liver oil, excessive vitamin-D enriched diet or calcium-rich diet and excessive radiation.

 

Vi-De 3 drops should only be prescribed with caution in patients who suffer from sarcoidosis as there is a risk of increased metabolism of vitamin-D to its active metabolite. In these patients, the calcium level should be monitored in urine and plasma.

 

Caution is required in patients with disturbances of calcium metabolism, kidney failure, kidney stones, arteriosclerosis or coronary disease. All forms of vitamin-D are toxic in high doses, causing a significant increase in calcium absorption.

 

 

In patients with renal impairment, vitamin-D and calcium salts should be taken under doctor’s supervision of the serum levels of calcium and phosphate.

 

In patients with severe renal insufficiency (patients on dialysis) vitamin-D is not regularly metabolized in the form of cholecalciferol, so other forms of vitamin-D should be given.

 

In case of highly dosed vitamin-D doses, monitoring of the patient is necessary and attention must be paid to the Sulkowich reaction in urine.

 

Toxic effects also occur if doses of 1000-3000 IU/kg bodyweight daily are administered over a period of several months. The fact that milk, fats and baby or other food are often enriched with vitamin-D should be taken into account when determining dosage.

Caution should be exercised in patients receiving treatment with cardiac glycosides since hypercalcaemia may lead to arrhythmia in such cases.

 

Particular care should be taken to avoid the following:

-        Prophylactic administration of vitamin-D in large doses over a period of months or even years without proper monitoring or a specific indication, especially in conjunction with calcium and/or vitamin enriched foods (e.g. baby foods).

-        Massive doses of vitamin-D at short intervals.

This is because the body’s attempts to eliminate the resultant calcium excess might overload the excretory capacity of the kidneys; the first symptoms of over-dosage are polyuria and polydipsia.

 

Patients receiving the recommended dose of Vi-De 3 for prevention of rickets or osteomalacia should avoid other drugs containing vitamin-D and vitamin-D enriched foods.

 

Particular care should be exercised in the prophylactic use of vitamin-D preparations in infants receiving vitamin-D enriched baby foods (normally containing 400 IU per daily portion).

 

Regular blood calcium checks should be performed on patients receiving long-term treatment for vitamin-D resistant rickets


Phenytoin, Phenobarbital barbiturates, glutethimide and rifampicin (and other liver-enzymes inducing agents) accelerate the degradation of cholecalciferol.

 

Cholestyramine, colestipol and mineral oils reduce the intestinal absorption of vitamin-D and as long an interval as possible (at least 4 hours) should therefore be allowed between ingestion.

 

There is an increased risk of hypercalcemia when vitamin-D is administered concurrently with thiazide diuretics, calcium or phosphate. Possibly necessitating the withdrawal of Vi-De-3.

 

The toxicity of cardiac glycosides may increase as a result of an increase in calcium levels during treatment with vitamin-D (risk of cardiac arrhythmia). Patients should be monitored by ECG and calcium levels in plasma and urine.

 

Functional antagonism with respect to inhibition and promotion of calcium desorption exists between corticosteroids and cholecalciferol.


Pregnancy:

There are no adequate data for the use of Vi-De 3 drops in pregnant women. However, animal experiments have shown that reproductive toxicity is associated with high doses of cholecalciferol.

There is clear evidence that doses in excess of the daily requirements represent a risk to the foetus.

Breast-feeding:

Since vitamin-D is excreted in the mother's milk, caution should be taken during breastfeeding


No available data.


Metabolism and nutrition disorders:

Undesirable effects are not to be expected when the dosage regulations are observed. The side effects arise as a result of overdosing. Symptoms of hypervitaminosis may occur incase of long-term overdose.

They manifest themselves in uncharacteristic complaints such as headache, loss of appetite, weakness, weight loss, stomach and intestinal disorders (emesis, constipation) and growth disorders.

In case of prolonged hypercalcemia, polyuria, polydipsia, nocturia, albuminuria, anorexia, hypercholesterolemia, elevated transaminase levels, cardiac arrhythmia, Hypertension and X-ray detectable soft tissue calcifications may occur.

Symptoms:

If vitamin-D over-dosage exceeds the excretory capacity of the kidneys, calcium deposits may be formed in the kidneys and vascular walls. The signs and symptoms of over-dosage are thirst, polyuria, loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, constipation, headache, joint pain, muscle weakness, dehydration (especially wizened, dry skin in children), tremor affecting the extremities, muscular atrophy with fibrillary contractions and hypertension. These symptoms are reversible provided vitamin-D is withdrawn in time.

 

Overdose leads to hypercalcemia and hypercalcuria. It can cause anorexia, nausea, vomiting, constipation, abdominal pain,  polydipsia, polyuria, nephrocalcinosis, kidney stones, severe cardiac arrhythmias and coma.

 

Treatment:

Immediate discontinuation of all vitamin-D intake, increase of fluid intake and avoidance of calcium-rich foods. Additionally in severe cases prednisolone 25 mg i.v., then 10 mg/day by mouth.

If the renal function is adequate, infusions of isotonic NaCl solution (3-6 L in 24 hours) with the addition of furosemide and in some cases also 15 mg/kg body weight/hour. Sodium edetate with continuous calcium and ECG control is quite reliable for calcium-lowering. On the other hand, haemodialysis therapy is indicated for oligo-anuria Glucocorticoids and calcitonin can be tried.


Pharmacotherapeutic group: Vitamin-D and analogues, cholecalciferol

ATC-Code: A11CC05

 

Vitamin-D 3 regulates both the calcium and phosphate as well as citric acid metabolism, promotes the intestinal absorption of calcium and controls the incorporation of calcium into the organic bone matrix.

 

Cholecalciferol is synthesized from 7-dehydrocholesterol in the skin when exposed to UV rays. In adults, this self-production is generally sufficient, but infants and toddlers often need an additional intake of vitamin-D 3 at our latitudes.


Cholecalciferol is absorbed via the gastrointestinal tract and transported into the liver. Cholecalciferol is also formed in the skin under the influence of UV rays. Cholecalciferol is hydroxylated for the first time in the liver, and a second time in the kidneys, producing mainly 1,25-dihydroxycolecalciferol, which is primarily responsible for vitamin-D action.

 

Vitamin-D is mainly stored in adipose tissue and skeletal muscle. The excretion occurs almost completely via faeces. Merely 2-4% is excreted via the urine. The excretion in humans is about 1-7   g/d, which corresponds approximately to the synthesis in the skin.

 

The half-life of cholecalciferol is up to 30 days.
