ANTIDIARRHEAL MICROORGANISMS.
ATC Code: A07FA02 (A: Digestive tract and metabolism).
FLORATIL is used as adjuvant treatment of diarrhea, in addition to rehydration in adults and children above 6 years.
 


If you have been told by your doctor that you have an intolerance to some sugars, contact your doctor before taking
this medicinal product.


DO NOT TAKE FLORATIL 200 mg capsule:
• If you are allergic to one of the ingredients of this medicine.
• If you have a central venous catheter, immunodeficiency or are hospitalized (because you are critically ill or have a
weakened immune system).
When in doubt, it is necessary to ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice.

Be careful with FLORATIL 200 mg capsule:
• This medicine is contraindicated in patients suffering from galactose intolerance, a Lapp lactase deficiency or
glucose-galactose malabsorption (rare hereditary diseases).
There have been reports in patients with a central venous catheter, critically ill or immunocompromised patients
even not treated with S. boulardii, of very rare cases of fungemia (penetration of blood by yeast), most often resulting
in pyrexia and blood cultures positive for Saccharomyces. In most cases, the outcome has been satisfactory after
cessation of treatment with Saccharomyces boulardii, administration of antifungal treatment and removal of the
catheter when necessary. However, the outcome was fatal in some critically ill patients. As with all medicines made
from living microorganisms, special attention must be paid to the handling of the product in the presence of patients
mainly with central venous catheter but also with peripheral catheter, even not treated with Saccharomyces boulardii,
in order to avoid any contamination by hand and/or the spread of microorganisms by air.
You must consult immediately your physician in the following cases:
• in absence of improvement after 2 days of treatment;
• in case of fever, vomiting;
• in case of presence of blood or mucus in the stools
• in case of intense thirst, dryness of tongue : these signs show the beginning of the dehydration, that means an
important loss of liquid due to the diarrhea. Your physician will decide the necessity to prescribe a rehydration which
could be administered by oral or IV route.

Precautions for use
As a complement of this treatment, the following dietetic rules have to be followed:
• rehydration by abundant, salted or sweetened drinks, in order to compensate for the loss of liquid due to the diarrhoea
(the average daily ration of water in adult is 2 litres);
• to feed during the diarrhoea:
- by excluding certain supply and particularly fruits, green vegetables, spiced dishes, as well as food or frozen drinks;
- by privileging roasted meats and rice.
FLORATIL contains living cells. This medicine should therefore not be mixed with very hot (over 50°C), iced or alcoholic
drinks or food.
When in doubt, do not hesitate to consult your physician or your pharmacist.

Taking other medicines
Do not use this medicine at the same time as an antifungal agent (medicine active against fungus infections).
Tell your doctor or your pharmacist if you are taking, have recently taken or might take any other medicines, including
medicines obtained without prescription.

Pregnancy and breastfeeding
Pregnancy
It is not recommended to take this medicine during pregnancy.
If you discover that you are pregnant during the treatment, consult your doctor as only he can decide if it is necessary
to go on the treatment.
Ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking this medicine.

Breastfeeding
Ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking this medicine.

Excipients with a recognized effect:
Lactose
 


Posology
Only in adults and children over 6 years.
1 capsule per day.
Mode and way of use
Oral route.
The capsules must be swallowed with a glass of water.
The lyophilisation of the active substance guarantees the great stability and vitality of Saccharomyces boulardii.
If you have any further questions on the use of this medicine, ask your doctor or pharmacist
 


Like all medicines, FLORATIL 200 mg capsules can cause side effects, although not everybody gets them.
Very rare (may affect up to 1 in 10,000 people):
• Some cases of hypersensitivity reactions (could be Quincke’s oedema), rash, itching, have been reported.
• Penetration of yeast into blood (fungaemia).
Rare (may affect up to 1 in 1,000 people): some cases of urticarial have been reported.
Frequency not known:
• Constipation
• Serious blood infection (sepsis)
 


Keep this medicine out of the sight and reach of children.
Do not take FLORATIL 200 mg capsule after the expiry date which is stated on the carton.
To be stored at a temperature below 30°C and away from humidity.
Do not throw away any medicines via wastewater or household waste. Ask your pharmacist how to throw away
medicines you no longer use. These measures will help protect the environment

Special warnings
In children above 6 years, if diarrhea persists after 2 days of treatment, management must be reviewed and
the need for rehydration using an oral or intravenous solution be envisaged.

This medicine contains lactose; its use is contra-indicated in patients suffering from an intolerance to
galactose, a lactase Lapp deficit or glucose and galactose malabsorption syndrome (rare hereditary
diseases).
There have been very rare cases of fungaemia (and blood cultures positive for Saccharomyces strains) and
sepsis reported mostly in patients with central venous catheter, critically ill or immunocompromised patients,
most often resulting in pyrexia. In most cases, the outcome has been satisfactory after cessation of treatment
by Saccharomyces boulardii, administration of antifungal treatment and removal of the catheter when
necessary. However, the outcome was fatal in some critically ill patients (see sections 4.3 and 4.8).
As with all medicines made from living micro-organisms, special attention must be paid to the handling of the
product in the presence of patients mainly with central venous catheter but also with peripheral catheter,
even not treated with Saccharomyces boulardii, in order to avoid any contamination by hands and/or the
spread of microorganisms by air (see section 4.2).
In addition, enteral administration of Floratil should be avoided because of the risk for environmental
contamination and cross-contamination when the seal of the capsule is opened.
Special precautions for use
Adults and children above 6 years old must be told of the need:
- to rehydrate themselves by drinking copious amounts of salty or sweet drinks, in order to
compensate for fluid losses due to diarrhea (mean daily water requirement of an adult is 2 liters);
- to eat while they have diarrhea,
 excluding certain types of food and especially raw salads, fruit, green vegetables, spicy
foods and iced foods or drinks,
 preferring grilled meats and rice.
Since FLORATIL consists of living cells that develop at 37°C: do not mix it with a liquid or food which is too
hot (more than 50°C), iced or containing alcohol.
 


Because of its fungal nature, FLORATIL must not be administered with systemic or oral antifungal drugs.
No other drug interactions are expected.
 


Pregnancy
Category C. Animal reproduction studies have not been conducted with FLORATIL.
Clinically, no malformative nor foetotoxic effect has been reported to date. However, the monitoring of
pregnancies exposed to this medicine is insufficient to rule out any risk.
Hence, as a precautionary measure, this medicine should not be used during pregnancy.
Breast-feeding
Saccharomyces boulardii is not absorbed. In the absence of data, it is preferable to weight benefit/risk before
using it during lactation.
 


Not relevant

Pharmaco-therapeutic class: Antidiarrheal microorganisms
Code ATC: A07F A02
A: Digestive tract and metabolism
Flora substitute.
Saccharomyces boulardii is an intestinal replacement flora, which acts as antidiarrhoel microorganisms in the
digestive tract. Pharmacodynamics of Saccharomyces boulardii, live probiotic yeast, have been established
on various models by in vitro and in vivo, animal, and human studies which demonstrates it has:
- anti-inflammatory (enterohemorrhagic E. coli)
- anti-microbial (enterohemorrhagic E. coli ; C. difficile; S. typhimurium ; Yersinia enterocolitica ;
C. albicans ; C. krusei ; C. pseudotropicalis)
- enzymatic (disaccharidases; leucine aminopeptidase; spermine; spermidine),
- metabolic,
- anti-toxinic (C. diff. toxin A; cholera toxin),
- and immune-enhancing (secretory-IgA and immunoglobulins) activities
which are beneficial when Saccharomyces boulardii is used to treat diarrhoea of various origins
(infectious-viral, bacterial- or not) in humans.
 


After repeated oral administration, Saccharomyces boulardii passes alive in the intestinal tract without
colonization.
Saccharomyces boulardii disappears rapidly from the stools, 2 to 5 days after the end of treatment.
