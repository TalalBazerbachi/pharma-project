  SFDA PIL (Patient Information Leaflet (PIL) are under review by Saudi Food and Drug Authority)

Dexipan cream contains the active ingredient dexpanthenol.

The active substance dexpanthenol is converted in the skin into the vitamin pantothenic acid and support the development and regeneration of the skin from within. The cream has soothing and cooling effect thanks to its high moisture content.

 Dexipan cream is used for treating overstressed and irritated but unbroken skin associated with:

•  Redness and sunburn

•  Pressure marks

•  Mild burns

•  Rough areas of skin (e.g. on the hands, elbows or feet)


You should not use Dexipan, if you are allergic (hypersensitive) to one or more of the ingredients in the product (see “what Dexipan contains?”)

Take special care with Dexipan in the following situations:

No special precautions are required provided that the product is used as directed. Inform your doctor or pharmacist, if you:

•  suffer from any other illnesses,

•  have allergies or,

•  are taking or extremely applying any other medicines (including those you purchase yourself)

Using Dexipan during pregnancy or while breast-feeding

Dexipan may be used during pregnancy and while breast-feeding.

Important information about some of the ingredients of Dexipan

Dexipan cream contains methyl paraben and propyl paraben. These may cause allergic reactions (possibly delayed).


Always use Dexipan exactly as your doctor has told you. You should check with your doctor or pharmacist if you are not sure.

Apply Dexipan once to several times daily as required.

Always use the dosage described in this leaflet or prescribed by your doctor. If you think that the effect of the medicine is too weak or too strong, please consult your doctor or pharmacist.

If you have any further questions on the use of this medicine, ask your doctor or pharmacist


The following side effects may occur when using dexpanthenol cream.

Side effects of dexpanthenol have only rarely been observed to date. Inform your doctor or pharmacist if you notice any unusual skin reactions.

If you notice any side effects not listed in this leaflet, please tell your doctor or pharmacist.

 

Reporting of side effects:

If you get any side effects, talk to your doctor, pharmacist or nurse. This includes any possible side effects not listed in this leaflet. You can also report side effects directly via:

• Saudi Arabia:

  The National Pharmacovigilance Centre (NPC):

-   Fax: +966-11-205-7662

-   SFDA Call Centre: 19999

-   E-mail: npc.drug@sfda.gov.sa

-   Website: https://ade.sfda.gov.sa

• Other GCC States:

-   Please contact the relevant competent authority

By reporting side effects, you can help provide more information on the safety of this medicine


-  Keep out of the reach and sight of children.

-  Do not use Dexipan after the expiry date which is stated on the carton and on the tube.

-  Store below 30°C. Best use within one month of opening.

-  Do not use Dexipan if you notice any visible sign of deterioration.

-  Medicines should not be disposed of via wastewater or household waste. Ask your pharmacist how to dispose of medicines no longer required. These measures will help to protect the environment.


What Dexipan contains

The active ingredient is Dexpanthenol.

Each 100gm of the cream contains 5gm of Dexpanthenol.

The other ingredients: White soft paraffin, cetostearyl alcohol, lanolin anhydrous, liquid paraffin, propylene glycol, methyl paraben, propyl paraben, tween 60, simethicone M30, lavender oil, rose oil, and purified water


What are the available packs of Dexipan Dexipan cream is available in packs containing collapsible tube of 20gm or pumps of 50 grams each. * Not all pack sizes may be marketed.
