- CalSource Ca-C1000 contains three active ingredients, two
are calcium salts (calcium lactate gluconate and calcium
carbonate) and the third is ascorbic acid (vitamin C).
– Calcium is an essential mineral, necessary for bone formation and
maintenance. Calcium also plays an important role in the way the
body functions. It is essential for nerve, muscle and heart function
as well as for clotting of blood. In addition, it mediates the action
of many hormones. To carry out these various roles, calcium must
be available to the appropriate tissues in the proper concentration.
– Vitamin C plays an important role in biochemical processes.
It is also needed for collagen formation (found in bone,
cartilage, skin, and other tissues) and tissue repair.
– CalSource Ca-C1000 effervescent tablets provide vitamin C and
calcium in amounts sufficient to cover the daily needs at times of
increased requirements.


A. Do not use CalSource Ca-C1000 if you:
– Are allergic (hypersensitive) to calcium lactate-gluconate,
calcium carbonate, vitamin C or any of the other ingredients
of this medicine (Listed in the end of this leaflet).
– Have high blood calcium levels (hypercalcemia).
– Have high calcium levels in urine (hypercalciuria).
– Have certain kidney problems including: kidney stones
(nephrolithiasis) and calcium deposits in your kidney tissue
(nephrocalcinosis).
B. Warnings and precautions
Talk to your doctor or pharmacist before using CalSource
Ca-C1000 if you:
– Have too much calcium in urine (hypercalciuria) with mild
or moderate impairment of kidney function or has a history
of stone formation, in these cases it is recommended to
increase the fluid intake. If necessary, the calcium dosage
will be reduced or therapy will be discontinued.
– Have high level of oxalic acid in urine.
– Suffer from a kidney disorder. In this case only use
this product after consulting your doctor or pharmacist
and especially if you are using aluminum containing
preparations, in case of kidney failure, consult your doctor or
pharmacist before using vitamin C.
High doses of vitamin D and derivatives should be avoided
during treatment with CalSource Ca-C1000 unless
recommended by your doctor.

C. Other medicines and CalSource Ca-C1000
Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are using or has recently
used or might use any other medicines.
– Thiazide diuretics (pills stimulating urine flow, used for
treatment of high blood pressure, or conditions where your
body accumulates too much fluid, such as heart failure).
– Systemic corticosteroids (anti-inflammatory drugs).
– Cardiac glycosides (digitalis containing medicines, used for
the treatment of heart failure).
– Tetracycline containing preparations (antibiotics). They should
be taken at least two hours before or four to six hours after oral
intake of CalSource Ca-C1000.
– Oral bisphosphonate (used for the treatment of bone problems) or sodium fluoride. They should be taken at least
three hours before the oral intake of CalSource Ca-C1000.
– Concomitant therapy with vitamin D and derivatives enhance
calcium absorption.
– Aluminium-containing antacids (which help to neutralize the
acid content of your stomach).
– Deferoxamine (used to remove excess iron from the body).
Do not take more than 1g of vitamin C as it may produce false
results in blood and urine tests (e.g for glucose determination).
D. CalSource Ca-C1000 with food and drink
The amount of calcium absorbed by your gut may be reduced
when eating certain foods, e.g. spinach, rhubarb and whole
cereals. You should not use calcium products within two hours
of eating such foods.
E. Driving and using machines
CalSource Ca-C1000 has no effect on the ability to drive and
use machines.
F. Important information about some of the ingredients of
CalSource Ca-C1000
This product contains:
– Sucrose: if you have been told by your doctor that you have
an intolerance to some sugars, contact your doctor before
taking this product.
– Sodium: this product contains 280 mg of sodium per dose.
To be taken into consideration by patients on a controlled
sodium diet.
Information for diabetics:
CalSource Ca-C1000 contains 2 g of sugar per tablet.


Always use this product exactly as described in this leaflet.
Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you are not sure.
Adults: the recommended dose is one effervescent tablet daily.
Dissolve the effervescent tablet in a glass of water (approx. 200
ml) and drink it immediately. CalSource Ca-C1000 effervescent
tablet may be taken with or without food.
If you use more CalSource Ca-C1000 than you should
Overdose may lead to high calcium levels in your urine
(hypercalciuria) and high blood calcium levels (hypercalcaemia).
Symptoms of high blood calcium levels (hypercalcaemia) may
include: nausea, vomiting, thirst, excess drinking, excess
urination, dehydration and constipation. Long term overdose
with increased calcium level in the blood can lead to calcium
deposits in the body. Large doses of vitamin C may cause
diarrhea and blood related disorders, such as hemolysis
(breakdown of red blood cells). Contact your doctor immediately
in case you have taken an overdose by mistake.
If you forget to take CalSource Ca-C1000
Do not take a double dose to make up for a forgotten dose.
If you have any further questions on the use of this product, ask your doctor
or pharmacist.


Like all pharmaceutical preparations, this product can cause side
effects, although not everybody gets them. STOP using CalSource
Ca-C1000 and seek medical help immediately if you have any of the
following which may be signs of an allergic reaction:
– Difficulty in breathing or swallowing.
– Swelling of the face, lips, tongue or throat.
– Severe itching of the skin, with a red rash or raised bumps.
These side effects are rare or very rare (may affect between 1 and 10
in every 10,000 people).
Some side effects are uncommon (may affect up to 1 in every
100 people):
– High calcium level in blood (hypercalcemia).
– High calcium level in urine (hypercalciuria).
Some side effects are rare (may affect up to 1 in every 10,000
patients):
– excessive gas in the stomach or gut (flatulence), constipation,
diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, rash, itching
(pruritus), hives (urticaria).
– Other possible side effects with unknown frequency
(frequency can not to be estimated from available data):
– Stone formation in the kidneys or urinary bladder (during
long-term treatment with high doses of calcium + vitamin C).
If you get any side effects, talk to your doctor or pharmacist.
This includes any possible side effects not listed in this leaflet.


– Store below 30 °C.
– Protect from Moisture, Heat and Light.
– Store in the original package. Keep the tube tightly closed.
– This medicine must not be used beyond the date indicated
after expiry date on the container.
– Keep out of reach and sight of children.
